# Interpol

Interpol is an American rock band formed in Manhattan in 1997 by guitarist Daniel Kessler, who recruited singer-guitarist Paul Banks, bassist Carlos Dengler, and drummer Greg Drudy (later replaced by Sam Fogarino) from his New York University circle. Their 2002 debut *Turn on the Bright Lights* — released in the same downtown moment as the [Strokes](./strokes.md)' *Is This It* and the [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./yeah-yeah-yeahs.md)' early EP — became a defining text of the [post-punk revival](../genres/post-punk-revival.md). Their sound, drawing openly from Joy Division and the Chameleons, gave the early-2000s [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) its moodiest soundtrack.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Kessler met Dengler at NYU in the [Greenwich Village](../neighborhoods/greenwich-village.md) area in 1997 and recruited Banks, then a film student. The band took its name from a 1953 film and its tailored-suit aesthetic from the Manchester post-punk era. They played their first shows at the Luna Lounge, the [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md), and the [Knitting Factory](../venues/knitting-factory.md) in 1998–99.

After several years of self-released EPs, the band signed to Matador Records in 2002. Their NYC base was a series of [East Village](../neighborhoods/east-village.md) and [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) practice spaces; Carlos Dengler's neat-suited bass posture and Banks's deadpan baritone became visual shorthand for the early-2000s downtown scene alongside [The Strokes](./strokes.md).

## Sound and signature work

*Turn on the Bright Lights* (2002) is a tightly-wound, reverb-soaked statement — "Obstacle 1," "PDA," and "NYC" — and its production by Peter Katis and Gareth Jones became its own template for a decade of indie rock. *Antics* (2004) refined the formula and produced "Slow Hands" and "Evil." *Our Love to Admire* (2007) was their first major-label record, on Capitol; their self-titled fourth (2010) preceded Carlos Dengler's departure.

*El Pintor* (2014) — an anagram of "Interpol" — was a successful three-piece reset, and *Marauder* (2018) and *The Other Side of Make-Believe* (2022) have kept them on a steady touring cycle, with a deluxe 20th-anniversary reissue of *Turn on the Bright Lights* in 2022.

## Manhattan venue history

- [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md) — early-career home venue.
- [Knitting Factory](../venues/knitting-factory.md) — late-'90s formative shows.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — *Bright Lights*-era breakthrough shows.
- Webster Hall — multiple headlining nights through the 2000s.
- Madison Square Garden — *Antics* / *Our Love to Admire*-era arena gigs.
- Forest Hills Stadium — recent New York-area headlining.

## Legacy

Interpol remains active as a touring trio — Banks, Kessler, and Fogarino — with periodic albums. Their direct stylistic influence on the second wave of post-punk-revival bands (from Editors to White Lies) is enormous. In the Manhattan story they are inseparable from the [Strokes](./strokes.md) / [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) / [LCD Soundsystem](./lcd-soundsystem.md) axis that briefly returned downtown rock to global relevance.

Carlos Dengler left music for film acting and writing in 2010, a memorable case of an indie-rock star walking away cleanly. Banks has released solo records and a project called Banks & Steelz with RZA of Wu-Tang Clan.
