# Yeah Yeah Yeahs

Yeah Yeah Yeahs are an American rock band formed in Manhattan in 2000 by singer Karen O (Karen Lee Orzolek), guitarist Nick Zinner, and drummer Brian Chase. They emerged from the same early-2000s [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) scene as [The Strokes](./strokes.md) and [Interpol](./interpol.md), but their sound — Zinner's wiry, effects-heavy guitar, Chase's elastic drums, and Karen O's full-throated, beer-throwing performance — pushed harder into art-damaged territory. Their 2003 debut *Fever to Tell* is one of the great rock records of its decade.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Karen O and Nick Zinner met at Oberlin College and reconnected in New York around 2000; Brian Chase, also an Oberlin alum, joined as drummer. Their first show was at the [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md) opening for the White Stripes in 2000 — a piece of band lore that became part of the early-2000s rock-revival narrative.

The band lived around the [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) and [East Village](../neighborhoods/east-village.md). Karen O's stage persona — torn dresses, paint-stained tights, microphone-eating, beer-spitting — was developed in tiny clubs like the [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md), Brownies, and Don Hill's. Their 2001 self-titled EP attracted UK press attention nearly instantly, and they were signed to Interscope shortly thereafter.

## Sound and signature work

*Fever to Tell* (2003) shifts between hyper-aggressive scrappers ("Date with the Night," "Black Tongue") and the unexpected ballad "Maps," whose video — Karen O crying real tears — became the band's defining image. *Show Your Bones* (2006) softened the edges and added acoustic textures; *It's Blitz!* (2009) traded guitars for synthesizers on the dance-floor anthem "Heads Will Roll."

*Mosquito* (2013) was a minor effort, and the band went into long hiatus. *Cool It Down* (2022), their first record in nine years, was a critically warm return featuring the lead single "Spitting Off the Edge of the World" with Perfume Genius — proof the trio remained creatively viable two decades into their career.

## Manhattan venue history

- [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md) — debut show in 2000 opening for the White Stripes; many follow-ups.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — multiple breakthrough-era shows in 2002–2003.
- Don Hill's (SoHo) — frequent early gigs in the venue's revival-rock heyday.
- Webster Hall — major NYC headlining nights through the 2000s.
- Madison Square Garden — co-headlining shows in the 2010s and 2020s.
- [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) — recording sessions for multiple albums.

## Legacy

The Yeah Yeah Yeahs remain active, though sporadic; Karen O has built a parallel career as a film composer (with Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross on *Where the Wild Things Are*) and solo artist. Nick Zinner has photographed extensively and produced other acts. Their imprint on later female-fronted rock — from St. Vincent to Wet Leg — is enormous.

In Manhattan, their lineage is intertwined with [The Strokes](./strokes.md), [Interpol](./interpol.md), and [LCD Soundsystem](./lcd-soundsystem.md) — all peers in the early-2000s [post-punk revival](../genres/post-punk-revival.md) that briefly made the [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) the center of the rock world for the second time in three decades.
