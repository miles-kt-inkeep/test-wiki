# The Strokes

The Strokes are an American rock band formed in 1998 in Manhattan by singer Julian Casablancas, guitarists Nick Valensi and Albert Hammond Jr., bassist Nikolai Fraiture, and drummer Fabrizio Moretti. Their 2001 debut *Is This It* effectively launched the global [post-punk revival](../genres/post-punk-revival.md), shifting the center of gravity of indie rock back to the [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) for the first time since the early-'80s [Sonic Youth](./sonic-youth.md) era. Their tight, downstroke-heavy sound — pulled equally from [Television](./television.md), the Velvet Underground, and the [Ramones](../genres/ramones.md) — defined a decade of New York rock.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Casablancas, Valensi, and Moretti met at the Dwight School on the Upper West Side in the mid-'90s; Hammond Jr. (son of the songwriter Albert Hammond) and Fraiture rounded out the lineup by 1998. The band rehearsed in basements and apartments around Manhattan and began playing the [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md) and [Bowery Ballroom](../venues/bowery-ballroom.md) in 1999.

Their 2001 EP *The Modern Age* set off a transatlantic bidding war won by RCA. The band became the figurehead of an early-2000s [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) scene that included [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), [Interpol](./interpol.md), TV on the Radio, and the Walkmen, dragging the music industry back to the same square-mile of Manhattan that [CBGB](../venues/cbgb.md) had occupied a generation earlier.

## Sound and signature work

*Is This It* (2001) — twelve tracks in 36 minutes — is the canonical record: tinny, deliberately compressed production, two interlocking guitars, Casablancas's distorted vocal mic'd through a tiny amp, and tracks ("Last Nite," "Hard to Explain," "Someday") engineered for repeat plays. *Room on Fire* (2003) refined the formula; *First Impressions of Earth* (2006) expanded and stretched it.

After a long hiatus and side projects, *Comedown Machine* (2013) and *The New Abnormal* (2020) brought them back; the latter won the Grammy for Best Rock Album, an unlikely capstone for a band once defined by downtown cool. Their most recent activity has been Casablancas's other group, the Voidz, and intermittent reunion shows.

## Manhattan venue history

- [Mercury Lounge](../venues/mercury-lounge.md) — early-career home venue, 1999–2001.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — first major NYC headlining gigs.
- Irving Plaza — *Is This It*-era victory laps.
- [CBGB](../venues/cbgb.md) — multiple shows during the venue's final years.
- Madison Square Garden — sold-out shows from 2011 onward.
- Forest Hills Stadium — late-period New York-area headlining.
- [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) — recorded portions of *Angles* and later albums.

## Legacy

The Strokes are still active, though they record sporadically. Their direct influence — tightly wound twin-guitar bands in skinny ties — is visible in the Arctic Monkeys, Franz Ferdinand, the Libertines, and a generation of British indie acts who took their cues from *Is This It*. In Manhattan their downstream is [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), [Interpol](./interpol.md), [LCD Soundsystem](./lcd-soundsystem.md), and [Vampire Weekend](./vampire-weekend.md), all of whom benefited from the rock-and-roll spotlight the Strokes briefly turned back onto downtown New York.
