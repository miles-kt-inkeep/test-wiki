# Patti Smith

Patti Smith is an American singer, songwriter, and poet whose 1975 debut album *Horses* is routinely placed at the very beginning of the Manhattan [punk rock](../genres/punk-rock.md) timeline. Originally from New Jersey, she arrived in New York in 1967 and lived for years at the Chelsea Hotel with photographer Robert Mapplethorpe before forming the Patti Smith Group in 1974. Her two-month residency with [Television](./television.md) at [CBGB](../venues/cbgb.md) in spring 1975 is widely credited with bringing critical and label attention to that venue and to downtown Manhattan rock as a whole. She remains one of the most respected living figures in American letters and music.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Smith moved to Manhattan in 1967 and gravitated to the bohemian [Chelsea](../neighborhoods/chelsea-meatpacking.md) milieu, working at Scribner's bookstore and writing poetry. She published her first book, *Seventh Heaven*, in 1972, and began performing poetry readings at St. Mark's Church in the [East Village](../neighborhoods/east-village.md), often accompanied by guitarist Lenny Kaye.

The Patti Smith Group — Smith, Kaye, pianist Richard Sohl, bassist Ivan Král, and drummer Jay Dee Daugherty — formed in 1974. Their seven-week joint residency with [Television](./television.md) at [CBGB](../venues/cbgb.md) in spring 1975 attracted Clive Davis, who signed Smith to Arista. She was a fixture at [Max's Kansas City](../venues/maxs-kansas-city.md) and circulated through the same loft scene as the [Ramones](../genres/ramones.md), [Blondie](./blondie.md), and [Talking Heads](./talking-heads.md).

## Sound and signature work

*Horses* (1975), produced by John Cale, opens with "Gloria" — a recitation ("Jesus died for somebody's sins but not mine") that detonates into a Van Morrison cover — and remains a foundational text for [punk rock](../genres/punk-rock.md). *Radio Ethiopia* (1976) was rougher; *Easter* (1978) gave her the Bruce Springsteen co-write "Because the Night," her only top-40 hit. *Wave* (1979) closed the original group's run before Smith retreated to Detroit with husband Fred "Sonic" Smith of the MC5.

She returned with *Gone Again* (1996) after Fred's death and has released steady, mature work since, including *Banga* (2012). Her memoir *Just Kids* (2010) won the National Book Award and is the definitive account of pre-CBGB Manhattan bohemia.

## Manhattan venue history

- [CBGB](../venues/cbgb.md) — landmark spring 1975 residency with [Television](./television.md); annual New Year's Eve / birthday shows for decades.
- [Max's Kansas City](../venues/maxs-kansas-city.md) — frequent appearances throughout the early-to-mid '70s.
- St. Mark's Church (East Village) — early '70s poetry readings.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — annual New York concerts in the 2000s and 2010s.
- [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) — recording sessions in the Greenwich Village studio Hendrix built.

## Legacy

Smith was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2007. She remains active as a touring artist and author, dividing her time between New York and travels abroad. Her influence on female rock vocalists — from PJ Harvey to Karen O of [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) to Florence Welch — is incalculable, and *Just Kids* has done more than any other single text to canonize the Chelsea Hotel and pre-punk [Lower East Side](../neighborhoods/lower-east-side.md) Manhattan in the public imagination.
