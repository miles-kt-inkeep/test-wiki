# Village Vanguard

The Village Vanguard is a basement jazz club at 178 Seventh Avenue South in [Greenwich Village](../neighborhoods/greenwich-village.md), opened by Max Gordon on February 22, 1935, and continuously operating ever since — making it the longest-running [jazz](../genres/jazz.md) club in the world. Its triangular 123-seat room has been the site of more landmark live recordings than any other venue in jazz history, from Sonny Rollins's *A Night at the Village Vanguard* (1957) to John Coltrane's 1961 Vanguard sessions to the Brad Mehldau Trio's *Live at the Village Vanguard* (2002 onward).

## Location and history

Max Gordon opened the Vanguard on February 22, 1935, in a basement space that had previously housed a speakeasy. The original programming was poetry, comedy, and folk, and only gradually shifted toward jazz through the 1940s. By 1957, when the club refocused entirely on jazz, it was already established as a listening room with strict rules: no talking, two sets a night, no exceptions.

Max Gordon ran the Vanguard until his death in 1989; his widow, Lorraine Gordon, took over and ran it until her death in 2018. Their daughter Deborah Gordon has run the club since.

## Scene and significance

The Vanguard's importance is hard to overstate: the Thad Jones / Mel Lewis Orchestra (now the Vanguard Jazz Orchestra) has played every Monday night since February 7, 1966, the longest-running residency in jazz. Sonny Rollins, John Coltrane, Bill Evans, Wynton Kelly, and Dexter Gordon all recorded definitive live albums at the club. The room is famously unforgiving acoustically — a detail that disciplined generations of musicians and produced the recordings that define modern [jazz](../genres/jazz.md).

## Notable performances / residencies

- Vanguard Jazz Orchestra (formerly Thad Jones / Mel Lewis) — every Monday night since 1966.
- Sonny Rollins — recorded *A Night at the Village Vanguard*, November 3, 1957.
- John Coltrane — recorded *Live at the Village Vanguard*, November 1–5, 1961.
- Bill Evans Trio — recorded the seminal Sunday sessions, June 25, 1961.
- Dexter Gordon — late-1970s comeback residencies.
- Brad Mehldau Trio — multiple recorded weeks since 2002.
- Fred Hersch — annual solo and trio runs that have produced multiple Grammy-nominated live albums.

## Today

The Village Vanguard is open and operating in 2026 under Deborah Gordon. The exterior storefront is part of the Greenwich Village Historic District designated in 1969; the basement room remains essentially as it was in the 1930s. It is widely regarded as the most important active jazz venue in the world and continues to host two sets per night, six nights a week.
