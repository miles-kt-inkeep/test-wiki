# Mudd Club

The Mudd Club was a four-story nightclub at 77 White Street in Tribeca, just south of [SoHo](../neighborhoods/soho.md), that operated from October 31, 1978, to 1983. Founded by filmmaker Steve Mass with curators Diego Cortez and Anya Phillips, it was the downtown counterweight to [Studio 54](./studio-54.md): explicitly anti-celebrity, art-driven, and cheap to get into if you looked right. The club incubated [no wave](../genres/no-wave.md), early hip-hop crossover events, and the visual-art scene that produced Basquiat and Haring.

## Location and history

Steve Mass and his partners opened the Mudd Club in a six-story cast-iron loft building owned by archivist Ross Bleckner; the club itself occupied the ground floor and gradually expanded upward. Named after Samuel Mudd, the doctor who treated John Wilkes Booth, the venue was deliberately positioned far from Midtown's glamour circuit.

A fourth-floor gallery curated by Keith Haring opened in 1981, mounting shows by Basquiat, Kenny Scharf, and Futura 2000. The Mudd Club closed in 1983 amid rising rents and competition from [Danceteria](./danceteria.md) and Area, both of which had absorbed many of its DJs and bookers.

## Scene and significance

The Mudd Club's live programming covered the full range of late-1970s downtown experimentation: [no wave](../genres/no-wave.md) bands like DNA and Mars, post-punk imports from the UK, early [hip-hop](../genres/hip-hop.md) showcases organized by Fab 5 Freddy, and one-night theme parties that mixed all three audiences. Talking Heads, Lydia Lunch, and the Lounge Lizards were regulars; the club is name-checked in songs by [Talking Heads](../artists/talking-heads.md) ("Life During Wartime") and Frank Zappa.

## Notable performances / residencies

- [Talking Heads](../artists/talking-heads.md) — multiple shows during the *Fear of Music* era.
- Lydia Lunch and Teenage Jesus and the Jerks — frequent no wave bills.
- The Cramps — multiple appearances during their NYC residency.
- Fab 5 Freddy — curator of early hip-hop nights bringing uptown crews downtown.
- Basquiat-as-musician with Gray — the painter's noise band performed repeatedly.
- Klaus Nomi — operatic performances that defined his visual identity.
- [Sonic Youth](../artists/sonic-youth.md) — earliest gigs in the no wave continuum.

## Today

The Mudd Club closed quietly in 1983. The building at 77 White Street is now residential condominiums. A small commemorative plaque designed in collaboration with the building's owners marks the site. The block was added to the Tribeca East Historic District in 1992, but the venue itself was never individually landmarked.
