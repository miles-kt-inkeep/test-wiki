# Paradise Garage

Paradise Garage was a private discotheque at 84 King Street in [SoHo](../neighborhoods/soho.md) that operated from 1977 to September 26, 1987. Built inside a former parking garage by Michael Brody, the club centered on resident DJ Larry Levan, whose 10-hour Saturday-night sets became the founding text for modern [electronic dance](../genres/electronic-dance.md). It was a Black, Latino, and gay-majority members club where the dance floor — not the door — was the point, and it functioned as the cultural counterweight to [Studio 54](./studio-54.md).

## Location and history

Michael Brody opened Paradise Garage in early 1977 with a series of "construction parties" while the soundsystem was still being assembled, and held the official opening on January 28, 1978. The space had previously been a working parking garage; the slope of the truck ramp at the entrance became iconic, as did the rooftop, where dancers cooled off at sunrise.

Membership cards distinguished Friday nights (mixed) from Saturday nights (predominantly gay men). The club closed on September 26, 1987, with a 24-hour final party; Brody died of AIDS-related illness later that year, and Larry Levan died in 1992.

## Scene and significance

The Garage's Richard Long-designed soundsystem and Larry Levan's programming established the template that house, garage, and modern club music still follow. The genre name "garage" is taken directly from the venue. Levan broke records by Loose Joints, Taana Gardner, and Class Action; his remix of Instant Funk's "I Got My Mind Made Up" was honed on the floor.

The club hosted live PAs by [disco](../genres/disco.md) and early house artists at the height of their careers, and an early-career [Madonna](../artists/madonna.md) performed there before her major-label breakthrough. Its influence runs through every subsequent New York dance institution from the Sound Factory to contemporary Brooklyn warehouses.

## Notable performances / residencies

- Larry Levan — resident DJ, every Saturday night for nearly a decade.
- [Madonna](../artists/madonna.md) — performed "Everybody" and "Holiday" before mainstream breakthrough.
- Grace Jones — multiple live PAs in the early 1980s.
- Diana Ross — surprise appearance and live performance in 1979.
- Patti LaBelle and Chaka Khan — featured live sets.
- Loose Joints — record-release party for "Is It All Over My Face."

## Today

Paradise Garage closed on September 26, 1987. The building at 84 King Street was used for years as a parking facility and event space; the original main room was demolished as part of redevelopment in the 2010s. The site was not landmarked. Larry Levan Way was designated by the City of New York in 2004 along the King Street block, and the annual Larry Levan Street Party still takes place there.
