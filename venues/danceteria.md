# Danceteria

Danceteria was a four-story nightclub that operated in Manhattan in three successive locations between 1979 and 1986, most famously at 30 West 21st Street in the [Chelsea / Meatpacking](../neighborhoods/chelsea-meatpacking.md) area. Founded by Rudolf Piper and Jim Fouratt, it offered a different musical genre on each floor — [new wave](../genres/new-wave.md) on one, [electronic dance](../genres/electronic-dance.md) on another, video lounge on a third — and is best remembered as the venue that gave [Madonna](../artists/madonna.md) her first crowd.

## Location and history

The first Danceteria opened in 1979 at 252 West 37th Street as an unlicensed after-hours club; it was raided and closed within a year. The second and most influential incarnation opened in 1980 at 30 West 21st Street, a former garment-industry building, and ran through 1986. A third location operated briefly on West 43rd Street.

Mark Kamins ran the third-floor dance room and championed disco-not-disco edits and early house imports; he produced [Madonna](../artists/madonna.md)'s first single, "Everybody," after spinning her demo at the club in 1982. Bookings by Ruth Polsky on the live-music floor brought the cream of the British post-punk and goth scenes to New York for their first US shows.

## Scene and significance

Danceteria was a node connecting [new wave](../genres/new-wave.md), [no wave](../genres/no-wave.md), [hip-hop](../genres/hip-hop.md), and the downtown art world. Sade played her first New York show there. The Smiths, New Order, the Cure, and Depeche Mode all played early US dates booked by Polsky. The [Beastie Boys](../artists/beastie-boys.md) played as a hardcore band; LL Cool J performed there in their hip-hop hours. Keith Haring and Jean-Michel Basquiat were regulars, and *Desperately Seeking Susan* filmed scenes inside the club.

## Notable performances / residencies

- [Madonna](../artists/madonna.md) — performed in 1982 before signing to Sire Records; Mark Kamins produced her debut single.
- Sade — US debut, 1984.
- The Smiths — first New York show, January 1984.
- New Order — early-1980s appearances on US tours.
- LL Cool J — performed in his teenage breakthrough year.
- [Beastie Boys](../artists/beastie-boys.md) — hardcore-era sets and DJ nights.
- Sonic Youth — early bills with no-wave alumni.

## Today

Danceteria closed in 1986 after a series of license disputes and the death of New York's loft-club era. The building at 30 West 21st Street has since housed offices and ground-floor retail. It was not landmarked. Several of the club's bookers and DJs went on to shape the next generation of New York venues, from Area to the Limelight.
