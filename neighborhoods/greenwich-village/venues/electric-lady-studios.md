# Electric Lady Studios

Electric Lady Studios is a recording complex at 52 West 8th Street in [Greenwich Village](../overview.md), commissioned by Jimi Hendrix and opened on August 26, 1970 — just 18 days before his death. Designed by acoustic engineer John Storyk in collaboration with producer Eddie Kramer, the curved-walled basement studio was the first major facility built by an artist for his own use, and it has remained one of the most sought-after recording rooms in the world across more than five decades.

## Location and history

The site at 52 West 8th Street was previously the Generation Club, a nightclub that Hendrix had bought with his manager Michael Jeffery in 1968 with the intention of running it as a venue. After mounting recording costs convinced Hendrix that he should own a studio rather than a club, the project was redesigned into Electric Lady. Construction took longer and cost more than expected — over $1 million by 1970 — but Hendrix recorded a substantial amount of *First Rays of the New Rising Sun* material there before his death on September 18, 1970.

After Hendrix's death the studio was sold to its staff in the 1970s. Subsequent ownership groups have maintained the original Storyk acoustic design and have continually upgraded the technical infrastructure.

## Scene and significance

Electric Lady has served almost every era of post-1970 popular music. Stevie Wonder cut *Talking Book* and parts of *Innervisions* there. [Patti Smith](../../../genres/punk-rock/artists/patti-smith.md) recorded *Horses* with John Cale at the studio in 1975, a foundational document of [punk rock](../../../genres/punk-rock/overview.md). The Clash worked on *Sandinista!* there in 1980. In the 21st century, the studio has been a near-constant home for [the Strokes](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md), [Vampire Weekend](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md), [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), and Daft Punk's *Random Access Memories*.

## Notable performances / residencies

- Jimi Hendrix — final sessions in summer 1970, including "Dolly Dagger" and "Freedom."
- Stevie Wonder — recorded large parts of *Talking Book* (1972) and *Innervisions* (1973).
- [Patti Smith](../../../genres/punk-rock/artists/patti-smith.md) — *Horses*, produced by John Cale, 1975.
- The Clash — *Sandinista!* sessions, 1980.
- [The Strokes](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md) — recorded portions of *Angles* and later albums in the 2010s.
- [Vampire Weekend](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md) — recorded *Modern Vampires of the City*, 2012–2013.
- Daft Punk — *Random Access Memories* string and live-band sessions, 2012.
- D'Angelo — *Voodoo* sessions, 1996–1999, anchoring the Soulquarians collective.

## Today

Electric Lady Studios is open and operating in 2026, owned and managed by a partnership that has held the building for several decades. The building is not individually landmarked but is part of the Greenwich Village Historic District. Tours are offered occasionally, and the studio continues to attract top-tier artists across rock, hip-hop, R&B, and electronic genres.
