# Blue Note (jazz club)

The Blue Note is a jazz nightclub at 131 West 3rd Street in [Greenwich Village](../overview.md), opened on September 30, 1981, by Danny Bensusan. With a capacity of roughly 200 across two nightly sets, it has hosted virtually every major living [jazz](../../../genres/jazz/overview.md) musician across more than four decades and is the anchor of the contemporary New York jazz-club circuit alongside the older [Village Vanguard](village-vanguard.md).

## Location and history

Danny Bensusan opened the Blue Note in a former bar on West 3rd Street between MacDougal and Sixth Avenue, just south of NYU. The room's tightly packed table layout — a deliberate echo of the old uptown supper-club model — supports two sets a night, plus a long-running late-night session that turned into a fertile testing ground for younger musicians.

The club expanded internationally beginning in 1988 with Blue Note Tokyo, followed by branches in Milan, Rio, Beijing, Hawaii, Napa, and elsewhere. The flagship on West 3rd Street remains the artistic center.

## Scene and significance

Where the [Village Vanguard](village-vanguard.md) is austere and listening-room-strict, the Blue Note is closer to a supper club — but its bookings are equally serious. Sarah Vaughan, Dizzy Gillespie, and Oscar Peterson played extended runs in the 1980s; Wynton Marsalis, Joe Lovano, and Ron Carter have been frequent presences across decades. The Blue Note has also hosted crossover bookings — Prince's late-career soundcheck-style residencies, Robert Glasper's genre-mixing nights — that broaden the definition of [jazz](../../../genres/jazz/overview.md) for new audiences.

## Notable performances / residencies

- Sarah Vaughan — multi-night residencies in the early 1980s.
- Dizzy Gillespie — 75th-birthday week in 1992.
- Oscar Peterson — annual returns through the 1990s.
- Chick Corea — long-running residencies recorded for several live albums.
- Wynton Marsalis — repeated bookings since the mid-1980s.
- Robert Glasper — Tuesday-night residency in the 2010s mixing jazz, hip-hop, and R&B.
- Prince — surprise late-night sets in 2013.

## Today

The Blue Note is open and operating in 2026 at its original West 3rd Street address. The building is not individually landmarked but lies within the Greenwich Village Historic District. Blue Note Entertainment Group also runs the adjacent Sony Hall and Le Poisson Rouge programming partnerships, and the international Blue Note rooms continue to expand.
