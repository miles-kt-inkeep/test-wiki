# Music Hall of Williamsburg

Music Hall of Williamsburg is a 550-capacity concert venue at 66 N. 6th Street in [Williamsburg](../overview.md), opened by Bowery Presents in October 2007 in the rebuilt space that previously housed Northsix (1999–2007). It is functionally the Brooklyn equivalent of the [Bowery Ballroom](../../lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — same operator, same booking philosophy, similar size, similar role as the room a band graduates to after [Mercury Lounge](../../lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md). Since opening it has been the most-used mid-size indie venue in Brooklyn.

## History

### Northsix (1999–2007)

The building's previous tenant, Northsix, was founded by Jeff Steinhauser and Jeff Hodgson and operated as Williamsburg's first dedicated indie-rock venue from 1999 to 2007. Through its eight years it was a critical proving ground for the early Williamsburg scene — bands like [TV on the Radio](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/tv-on-the-radio.md), Animal Collective, Yeah Yeah Yeahs, and the early-2000s wave of Brooklyn-affiliated indie acts played the room repeatedly. Its 350-capacity size and DIY booking style fit the pre-2007 neighborhood. Northsix closed in 2007 when the operators sold the building to Bowery Presents.

### Music Hall of Williamsburg (2007–present)

Bowery Presents (Manhattan-based concert promoter operating [Bowery Ballroom](../../lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md), [Mercury Lounge](../../lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md), and Webster Hall under contract) gutted the building, expanded the capacity to 550, added a balcony, and reopened in October 2007 as Music Hall of Williamsburg. The venue's first night was a Maxïmo Park show; within months it had become the borough's flagship indie-rock room. Its booking has tracked Bowery Ballroom's closely — many bands play both rooms in successive nights on Brooklyn-and-Manhattan tours.

The room's acoustics, sightlines, and capacity make it the room every touring act of a certain size wants to play in Brooklyn. The list of artists who have played career-defining sets here through the 2010s and 2020s includes [Grizzly Bear](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/grizzly-bear.md), [Dirty Projectors](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/dirty-projectors.md), the National, Vampire Weekend (early career), Sharon Van Etten, Mitski, Phoebe Bridgers, and a long list of smaller indie bands.

## Building and layout

66 N. 6th Street sits a half-block off Bedford Avenue, the L train, and the Williamsburg waterfront. The room is rectangular with a stage at the south end, a horseshoe balcony, and a small bar at the back. The 550-capacity number is a mix of 350 standing on the floor plus 200 in the balcony.

## Place in the wiki

Music Hall of Williamsburg is the Brooklyn entry in the same conceptual slot the [Bowery Ballroom](../../lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) occupies on the Manhattan side: a mid-size, well-run, well-booked indie-rock room operated by Bowery Presents, with a clear ladder relationship below it ([Mercury Lounge](../../lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) on the LES, [Baby's All Right](https://babysallright.com) on the Williamsburg / South-Williamsburg border) and above it ([Brooklyn Steel](../../bushwick/venues/brooklyn-steel.md) since 2017). It is the room in which the early-2010s [post-punk revival](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/overview.md) afterglow and the broader Brooklyn-indie wave have done much of their public work.
