# Brooklyn Bowl

Brooklyn Bowl is a concert venue, bowling alley, restaurant, and bar at 61 Wythe Avenue in [Williamsburg](../overview.md), opened in July 2009 by Peter Shapiro and Charley Ryan in a converted iron foundry. With a 600-capacity concert floor, 16 bowling lanes, and a Blue Ribbon-operated kitchen, it has been Brooklyn's quirkiest mid-size live-music room since opening — a place where the bowling and the concerts genuinely run simultaneously without ruining either.

## History

Peter Shapiro had previously operated the Wetlands Preserve in Tribeca (1989–2001), a hippie-and-jam-leaning Manhattan club whose closing left a gap that Brooklyn Bowl partly filled. The Wythe Avenue building — a 23,000-square-foot former iron foundry — was extensively retrofitted (LEED-certified construction, in-floor concert sound) before opening in July 2009. The first night featured a six-hour set by ?uestlove, who would go on to hold a long-running Bowl Train residency at the venue; that residency is the closest thing the venue has to a defining tradition.

The venue's booking is more eclectic than the indie-focused [Music Hall of Williamsburg](./music-hall-of-williamsburg.md) two blocks away. Funk, jam, soul, hip-hop, indie, and DJ nights all rotate; the building is also routinely rented for private events and TV tapings.

The success of the Williamsburg flagship led to expansion: Brooklyn Bowl Las Vegas (2014), Brooklyn Bowl Nashville (2020), and Brooklyn Bowl Philadelphia (2020). The Williamsburg location remains the original and the one most-cited.

## Building and layout

61 Wythe Avenue is on the block bounded by Wythe, North 11th, North 12th, and Berry, two blocks from the East River and one block from Bedford Avenue. The 23,000-square-foot interior is configured as a great-room concert floor (600 capacity standing) with the bowling lanes along one side wall, the bar at the back, and Blue Ribbon's kitchen feeding the floor. The acoustics — designed by John Storyk's Walters-Storyk Design Group — are unusually good for a venue with bowling lanes running through it.

## Place in the wiki

Brooklyn Bowl sits in a different conceptual slot from [Music Hall of Williamsburg](./music-hall-of-williamsburg.md). MHoW is the indie-rock graduation room; Brooklyn Bowl is the borough's eclectic mid-size venue — the room you book when the act is too funk-and-jam, too hip-hop, or too cross-genre for the strict indie-rock audience. ?uestlove's long-running residency, frequent J Dilla / Roots-adjacent programming, and the Blue Ribbon food draw a different crowd from Music Hall's. The two rooms together have anchored Williamsburg's 2010s and 2020s mid-size live-music economy.
