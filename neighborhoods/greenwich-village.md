# Greenwich Village

Greenwich Village runs from Houston Street north to 14th Street, and from Broadway west to the Hudson River, with the West Village proper west of Sixth (Avenue of the Americas). It is the oldest continuously musical neighborhood in Manhattan: the 1960s folk revival broke here, the great postwar [jazz](../genres/jazz.md) clubs lined Seventh Avenue South, and the Village absorbed a tail end of the Velvet Underground / Warhol crowd as it spilled out of the [East Village](./east-village.md). Even after the rock action moved south to the [Lower East Side](./lower-east-side.md) in the late 1970s, the Village kept its jazz rooms and its recording studios.

## Geography and character

Washington Square Park is the geographic and emotional center — a bowl of NYU buildings, chess tables, and folk singers since the 1950s. Bleecker and MacDougal Streets, the spine of the 1960s folk scene, still run through the south end of the neighborhood. Seventh Avenue South cuts diagonally across the Village and is the jazz corridor: the [Village Vanguard](../venues/village-vanguard.md) has been at 178 Seventh Avenue South since 1935, and the [Blue Note](../venues/blue-note.md) opened at 131 West 3rd in 1981. West 8th Street, where Hendrix built [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) in 1970, is the Village's most musically consequential block of pavement.

## Music history

In the early 1960s, the Village was the unquestioned American capital of folk. Cafe Wha?, Gerde's Folk City, the Bitter End, and the Gaslight Cafe stretched along Bleecker and MacDougal; Bob Dylan walked off a bus from Minnesota in 1961 and was playing Cafe Wha? within days. By mid-decade the Village had launched Joan Baez, Simon and Garfunkel, Phil Ochs, and Dave Van Ronk, and provided the backdrop for the more bohemian end of the Velvet Underground-adjacent Warhol crowd — though most of that scene actually lived east, around [Max's Kansas City](../venues/maxs-kansas-city.md).

The jazz tradition at the [Village Vanguard](../venues/village-vanguard.md) ran continuously through every decade: Coltrane recorded *Live at the Village Vanguard* there in 1961, Bill Evans in 1961, Sonny Rollins in 1957. The [Blue Note](../venues/blue-note.md), opening in 1981, gave Greenwich Village a second great jazz room and made the neighborhood once again a destination for the form.

In 1970, Jimi Hendrix opened [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) at 52 West 8th Street; he died weeks later, but the studio became one of the most important rooms in American recording. [Patti Smith](../artists/patti-smith.md) cut *Horses* there in 1975; decades later, [the Strokes](../artists/strokes.md) and others returned to record in the same building, knitting the Village to the [Lower East Side](./lower-east-side.md) revival.

By the 2010s the Village was an expensive landmark district with a steady flow of jazz pilgrims, NYU students, and recording sessions, but the rock and folk scenes had long since moved on.

## Key venues

- [Village Vanguard](../venues/village-vanguard.md) — 178 Seventh Avenue South; America's most storied jazz club, open since 1935.
- [Blue Note](../venues/blue-note.md) — 131 West 3rd Street; the modern jazz anchor, opened 1981.
- [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) — 52 West 8th Street; Hendrix-built recording studio, still active.
- Cafe Wha? — 115 MacDougal; Dylan's first New York stage.
- The Bitter End — 147 Bleecker; the longest-running rock and folk club in the city.

## Notable artists and acts

- [Patti Smith](../artists/patti-smith.md) — recorded *Horses* at Electric Lady in 1975.
- [The Strokes](../artists/strokes.md) — recorded multiple albums at Electric Lady.
- [Television](../venues/television.md) — Verlaine and Hell met in Village rooming houses before the CBGB years.
- [The New York Dolls](../artists/new-york-dolls.md) — early Mercer Arts Center residencies in 1972 sat just east of the Village line.

## Today

Greenwich Village is the most preserved and most expensive of Manhattan's musical neighborhoods. NYU has absorbed most of the building stock south of Washington Square; brownstones in the West Village trade for $10 million. The [Village Vanguard](../venues/village-vanguard.md), [Blue Note](../venues/blue-note.md), and [Electric Lady Studios](../venues/electric-lady-studios.md) are still in their original buildings, which makes the Village an outlier — most of music's other Manhattan neighborhoods have lost their landmark rooms. The folk circuit on Bleecker has thinned to one or two holdouts, and most up-and-coming acoustic acts now play Brooklyn instead.
