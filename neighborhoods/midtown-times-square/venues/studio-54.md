# Studio 54

Studio 54 was a nightclub at 254 West 54th Street in [Midtown / Times Square](../overview.md), open as the original disco from April 26, 1977, to February 4, 1980. Founded by Steve Rubell and Ian Schrager, it became the global symbol of [disco](../../../genres/disco/overview.md) culture, paparazzi-driven celebrity, and the velvet-rope door policy that excluded as much as it admitted. Its converted-theater interior, with the famous moon-and-coke-spoon scenery hovering over the dance floor, has been imitated worldwide.

## Location and history

The building began life in 1927 as the Gallo Opera House and later operated as the Casino de Paris and, from 1942, as CBS Studio 52, where *What's My Line?* and *The $64,000 Question* were taped. Rubell and Schrager bought the lease in late 1976, kept the theatrical infrastructure (the catwalks, the trap rooms, the working fly system), and renamed it Studio 54 after the street.

The original club's run ended on February 4, 1980, when Rubell and Schrager went to prison for tax evasion; their farewell party drew Diana Ross, Liza Minnelli, and Sylvester. The club briefly reopened in 1981 under new ownership but never recaptured its cultural moment, and shuttered as a nightclub in 1986.

## Scene and significance

Studio 54 was the apex of mainstream [disco](../../../genres/disco/overview.md) in New York, in the same era that [Paradise Garage](../../soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) was developing the deeper, gay, Black, and Latino sound that would feed [electronic dance](../../../genres/electronic-dance/overview.md) for decades. Where the Garage was a private club for serious dancers, 54 was a media spectacle: Bianca Jagger on a white horse, Truman Capote at the bar, Andy Warhol at a banquette.

DJs Richie Kaczor and Nicky Siano set the early sonic template; Kaczor's championing of Gloria Gaynor's "I Will Survive" turned the song into the disco anthem. The club hosted album-release parties, fashion shows, and the occasional live set — including a 1977 performance by Grace Jones and a famous birthday party for Bianca Jagger.

## Notable performances / residencies

- Grace Jones — performed in 1977 and 1978, helping define the club's drama.
- Diana Ross — sang at the closing-night party, February 4, 1980.
- Sylvester — closing-night appearances and frequent guest spots.
- Richie Kaczor — resident DJ whose sets are widely bootlegged.
- Liza Minnelli, Bianca Jagger, and Andy Warhol — fixtures whose presence turned ordinary nights into events.
- The Village People — promotional performances at the height of "Y.M.C.A."

## Today

The original disco closed as a nightclub in 1986. The building at 254 West 54th Street was converted into a Broadway venue and reopened in 1998 as Studio 54, a theater operated by the Roundabout Theatre Company; it continues to host plays and musicals today. The interior was substantially preserved and renovated. The exterior is part of no formal landmark district, though the venue is widely treated as a cultural landmark.
