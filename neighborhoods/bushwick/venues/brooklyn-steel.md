# Brooklyn Steel

Brooklyn Steel is an 1,800-capacity concert venue at 319 Frost Street in East Williamsburg / [Bushwick](../overview.md), opened by Bowery Presents in April 2017 in a converted 27,000-square-foot former metal-fabrication warehouse. From its opening it has been the most-booked venue at its size in New York City — replacing both Manhattan's Terminal 5 (less acoustically liked, harder for fans to reach) and various competing Brooklyn rooms as the default room for arena-track touring acts on a club night.

## History

Bowery Presents had been looking for a Brooklyn venue at this size since the mid-2010s, when its existing portfolio (the 575-cap [Bowery Ballroom](../../lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md), the 250-cap [Mercury Lounge](../../lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md), the 550-cap [Music Hall of Williamsburg](../../williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md)) topped out below the size touring agents needed for the next step in an act's career. The 319 Frost Street building — a former Robert Lehrer Sons metal fabrication warehouse — was acquired and converted under a Madison Square Garden Entertainment partnership; Bowery Presents had been acquired by AEG/MSG in 2014.

The venue opened on April 6, 2017 with a sold-out LCD Soundsystem residency — eight nights, the band's first New York shows since their 2011 "breakup." The booking choice was deliberately symbolic: [LCD Soundsystem](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md), the Brooklyn-emblematic band of the previous decade, opening Brooklyn's biggest new club. From there the booking calendar filled immediately. Through 2017–2024 the room hosted (an incomplete list) the National, Phoebe Bridgers, Mitski, Alvvays, the War on Drugs, Vince Staples, Earl Sweatshirt, Soccer Mommy, and most major touring indie / hip-hop / electronic acts at the 1,500–2,500 attendance level.

## Building and layout

319 Frost Street sits in the East Williamsburg industrial pocket — closer to the Morgan L stop than the Bedford one, bordered by working warehouses and the Newtown Creek industrial zone. The 27,000-square-foot interior is configured as a single large-room standing floor with a sound booth at the back, no balcony, and high ceilings that the original metal-fabrication use required. PK Sound rigged the room; the acoustics are notably better than typical for a converted warehouse.

The location's downside is access — there is no convenient subway exit, and walking from the Morgan L stop is roughly 12 minutes through industrial blocks. After-show car-service pickups along Frost Street are part of the venue's standard friction.

## Place in the wiki

Brooklyn Steel slots into the venue ladder above [Music Hall of Williamsburg](../../williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md) and [Bowery Ballroom](../../lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md), and below the genuinely-arena-scale rooms (Barclays Center in Prospect Heights, Madison Square Garden in [Midtown](../../midtown-times-square/overview.md)). Its opening in 2017 marked the moment when Brooklyn became the institutional center of mid-large live music in New York rather than a neighborhood-DIY counterweight to Manhattan. Its first eight nights — the LCD Soundsystem residency — are the closest the venue has to a founding myth.
