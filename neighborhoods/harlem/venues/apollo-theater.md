# Apollo Theater

The Apollo Theater stands at 253 West 125th Street in [Harlem](../overview.md) and has been a flagship for Black American music since January 26, 1934. Its weekly Amateur Night, founded that year, has launched the careers of Ella Fitzgerald, James Brown, the Jackson 5, Lauryn Hill, and many more. Across nearly a century the Apollo has cycled through [jazz](../../../genres/jazz/overview.md), R&B, soul, and [hip-hop](../../../genres/hip-hop/overview.md) without losing its central place in Harlem's cultural identity.

## Location and history

The building opened as Hurtig & Seamon's New Burlesque Theater in 1914 and operated as a whites-only burlesque house through the early 1930s. After Mayor Fiorello La Guardia's crackdown on burlesque, Sidney Cohen and Morris Sussman re-opened it as the Apollo on January 26, 1934, expressly programming for Harlem's Black audience. Frank Schiffman and Leo Brecher acquired the theater in 1935 and ran it for decades.

The Apollo declined in the 1970s as 125th Street faced disinvestment, and closed briefly in 1976. After several reopenings it was acquired by the State of New York in 1981 and turned over to a nonprofit foundation in 1991. The Apollo Theater Foundation has run it since.

## Scene and significance

The Apollo's "Amateur Night at the Apollo" — currently in its tenth decade — is the longest-running talent showcase in American popular music. Ella Fitzgerald won her first contest in 1934; Billie Holiday, Sarah Vaughan, James Brown, Stevie Wonder, Gladys Knight, and Lauryn Hill all passed through its rounds. The *Live at the Apollo* recordings by James Brown (1962) and Aretha Franklin's 1971 sessions are foundational documents of Black music. In the 21st century the room has hosted hip-hop showcases for [A$AP Mob](../../../genres/hip-hop/artists/asap-mob.md) and other Harlem-rooted artists.

## Notable performances / residencies

- Ella Fitzgerald — Amateur Night winner, November 21, 1934.
- James Brown — *Live at the Apollo* recorded October 24, 1962.
- The Jackson 5 — Amateur Night appearance in 1967, before Motown.
- Lauryn Hill — Amateur Night performance in 1987, age 13.
- Aretha Franklin — multiple late-career engagements; Apollo memorial in 2018.
- [A$AP Mob](../../../genres/hip-hop/artists/asap-mob.md) — annual hometown showcases in the 2010s.
- Bruce Springsteen — 2012 SiriusXM concert that made wide news.

## Today

The Apollo Theater is open and operating in 2026 under the Apollo Theater Foundation. It was designated a New York City landmark in 1983 and added to the National Register of Historic Places the same year. A multi-year renovation and capital campaign expanded the campus in the early 2020s with new performance spaces on West 126th Street.
