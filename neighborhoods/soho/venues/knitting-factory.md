# Knitting Factory

The Knitting Factory was an experimental music club founded by Michael Dorf and Bob Appel that operated in Manhattan from February 1987 until 2009, moving across three Manhattan addresses before relocating to Brooklyn. Its first home at 47 East Houston Street and its long-running Tribeca location at 74 Leonard Street made it the central node for downtown [jazz](../../../genres/jazz/overview.md), [no wave](../../../genres/no-wave/overview.md), and the broader experimental music ecosystem associated with John Zorn's Knitting Factory Works label.

## Location and history

Dorf and Appel opened the original Knitting Factory on February 12, 1987, on the second floor of 47 East Houston Street, a former Avon office. The room booked seven nights a week — jazz, poetry, indie rock, and free improvisation — on a tiny budget. By 1994 the club had outgrown the space and moved to 74 Leonard Street in Tribeca, expanding to four performance rooms (the Main Space, the Tap Bar, the Old Office, and the Alterknit Theater).

The Tribeca location was the club's apex; it ran John Zorn's residencies, the annual *What Is Jazz?* festival, and televised concerts. The Manhattan story effectively ended in 2008–2009 when the club was sold and reopened as a smaller Williamsburg venue at 361 Metropolitan Avenue. The Tribeca space closed for good in 2009.

## Scene and significance

The Knitting Factory was the public face of the so-called "Downtown" scene that orbited John Zorn, Bill Frisell, and the musicians documented on the Tzadik label. It also booked rock — early shows by [Sonic Youth](../../../genres/no-wave/artists/sonic-youth.md) collaborators, [Beastie Boys](../../../genres/hip-hop/artists/beastie-boys.md) side projects, and the Ween-adjacent weirder end of the 1990s indie scene — as well as hip-hop showcases and electronic nights. Its programming routinely linked free [jazz](../../../genres/jazz/overview.md) lineages from the [Village Vanguard](../../greenwich-village/venues/village-vanguard.md) tradition to the [no wave](../../../genres/no-wave/overview.md) descendants who came of age at the [Mudd Club](mudd-club.md).

## Notable performances / residencies

- John Zorn — long-running residencies, including Naked City and Masada launches.
- [Sonic Youth](../../../genres/no-wave/artists/sonic-youth.md) — solo and collaborative experimental sets.
- Bill Frisell, Marc Ribot, and Wayne Horvitz — anchor improvisers across two decades.
- The Lounge Lizards — multi-night runs in the Tribeca era.
- Cecil Taylor — late-career performances at the *What Is Jazz?* festival.
- Medeski Martin & Wood — early-1990s residency that built their audience.
- Yoko Ono — multiple appearances during the Tribeca era.

## Today

The original Houston Street and Leonard Street rooms have both closed; both buildings remain standing but house unrelated tenants. The Knitting Factory brand continued in Brooklyn at 361 Metropolitan Avenue from 2009 to 2020 and has since opened smaller rooms in Boise and Spokane. Neither Manhattan venue was landmarked. Knitting Factory Records continued to operate through the 2010s and has issued reissues of the club's seminal live recordings.
