# Saint Vitus

Saint Vitus is a 250-capacity bar and concert venue at 1120 Manhattan Avenue in [Greenpoint](../overview.md), opened in 2011 by David Castillo, George Souleidis, Arthur Shepherd, and Tom Lipnick. Within two years of opening it was internationally recognized as one of the most important small metal-and-hardcore rooms in the world; through the 2010s and 2020s it has hosted formative U.S. shows by an unusually long list of underground metal, doom, sludge, hardcore, post-rock, and noise acts.

## History

The four founders had backgrounds in New York metal and hardcore (Castillo's Black Anvil; Shepherd's Defeatist; Souleidis as a longtime promoter) and opened Saint Vitus with the explicit intent of running a small venue that took underground heavy music seriously rather than booking it as a side category. The name comes from the doom-metal band Saint Vitus.

The room consists of a bar at the front and a separate concert space at the back, accessible through a small archway. Capacity is roughly 250 standing. The acoustics, sightlines, and (unusually) low ceiling all suit the heavy-music programming the venue specializes in; the bar's dark, cathedral-leaning aesthetic became part of the venue's broader brand.

By 2013 — two years in — Saint Vitus was being treated by the metal press (*Decibel*, *Invisible Oranges*, *MetalSucks*) as one of the world's small set of essential heavy-music venues. The list of bands that played first or formative U.S. shows there from 2011 to 2024 includes Pallbearer (Arkansas doom), Tribulation (Sweden), Power Trip (Texas thrash), Deafheaven (San Francisco black-metal-shoegaze hybrid), Sumac (Aaron Turner's post-Isis project), Krallice (the New York black-metal flagship — Saint Vitus is functionally their hometown venue), Liturgy, Yob, Bell Witch, and dozens more. The venue also hosts hardcore (Trapped Under Ice, Code Orange), post-rock (Russian Circles), and noise / experimental bookings.

A late-2024 / early-2025 ownership transition was reported in the local press; the venue has continued operating but the founder group has changed. Coverage of that transition is incomplete in this wiki and would benefit from a follow-up update.

## Building and layout

1120 Manhattan Avenue sits at the northern end of Greenpoint's commercial spine, two blocks from the Greenpoint Avenue G stop. The single-story storefront is unmarked from the street except for a small sign — typical of north-Brooklyn DIY-adjacent venues. Inside, the front bar is dimly lit and decorated with metal paraphernalia; the back room (where shows happen) is even darker, with a small raised stage and the floor staying flat to the back wall.

## Place in the wiki

Saint Vitus is the wiki's only metal-and-hardcore venue page, which reflects rather than distorts the broader picture: New York's metal-and-hardcore scene has long had small, fragmented homes (CB's 313 Gallery and ABC No Rio in earlier eras, Knitting Factory's heavy nights, various Brooklyn lofts) without a single dominant institutional venue at any size. Saint Vitus, opening 2011, became that institutional venue for the genre's underground-tier touring economy. It is the closest thing the wiki's Brooklyn coverage has to a CBGB analogue — small, scene-defining, run by people from the scene itself.
