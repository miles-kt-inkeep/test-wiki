# Mercury Lounge

The Mercury Lounge is a small concert venue at 217 East Houston Street in the [Lower East Side](../overview.md), open since September 1993 and operated by Bowery Presents. With a capacity of roughly 250, it is the canonical "first headlining show in Manhattan" venue, a feeder room whose graduates routinely move on to the [Bowery Ballroom](bowery-ballroom.md) and beyond. Almost every important New York band of the past three decades has played there.

## Location and history

Michael Swier and partners opened the Mercury Lounge in September 1993 inside a former tombstone showroom on Houston Street between Ludlow and Essex. The narrow main room, with the bar in front and the stage at the back, established the template for the small-club rebirth of the [Lower East Side](../overview.md) in the 1990s.

The Mercury and the [Bowery Ballroom](bowery-ballroom.md) were initially independent operations under Swier; both merged into Bowery Presents in 2004, which itself was acquired by AEG in 2017. The Mercury has remained almost continuously open since 1993, with a brief shutdown during the 2020 pandemic.

## Scene and significance

The Mercury Lounge's bookings define the New York indie pipeline. [The Strokes](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md) played their early 2000 and 2001 shows there before moving up to the Bowery; [Interpol](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md), [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), and [LCD Soundsystem](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) all worked the room into a sweat before their major-label debuts. Outside the [post-punk revival](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/overview.md), the room has hosted hip-hop showcases, alt-country anchors like Lucinda Williams's surprise appearances, and early-career sets by Sharon Van Etten and Mitski.

## Notable performances / residencies

- [The Strokes](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md) — Tuesday-night residency in early 2001 widely credited with the band's takeoff.
- [Interpol](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md) — early 2001–2002 shows that drew Matador's interest.
- [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) — multiple breakout shows in 2002.
- [LCD Soundsystem](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) — 2002 DFA-era debut shows.
- [Vampire Weekend](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md) — 2007 sold-out shows pre-debut album.
- Jeff Buckley — multiple 1993–1994 sets in the venue's earliest months.
- Lana Del Rey — 2011 industry showcase pre-*Born to Die*.

## Today

The Mercury Lounge is open and operating in 2026 under AEG/Bowery Presents. The building is not individually landmarked, though it sits within the broader Lower East Side cultural landscape. The room has been only modestly renovated since 1993, preserving its narrow, sweat-soaked character.
