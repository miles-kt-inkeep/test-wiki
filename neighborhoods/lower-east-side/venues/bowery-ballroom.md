# Bowery Ballroom

The Bowery Ballroom is a concert venue at 6 Delancey Street, a half-block from the Bowery in the [Lower East Side](../overview.md), that opened in 1998. Operated by the Bowery Presents collective, it has a capacity of roughly 575 and is widely considered the most acoustically generous mid-size room in New York. It became the launchpad for the early-2000s [post-punk revival](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/overview.md) and remains a near-mandatory stop for indie acts moving from clubs to theaters.

## Location and history

The building at 6 Delancey Street was constructed in 1929 as a retail space — originally an upscale shoe store — and sat largely vacant from the 1970s onward. John Moore and Brian Swier renovated the space and opened the Bowery Ballroom on March 27, 1998, with a debut performance by Joe Jackson. The two-tiered design (a main floor with a balcony U-shaped around it) allows clear sightlines from anywhere in the room, a rarity in New York's club inventory.

The Bowery Presents organization, which grew up around the room, also operates the [Mercury Lounge](mercury-lounge.md), Webster Hall, and other regional venues; the Mercury–Bowery pipeline is the standard career arc for New York-based bands.

## Scene and significance

In the early 2000s the Bowery Ballroom was the room where the [post-punk revival](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/overview.md) bands consolidated their audiences: [the Strokes](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md) played sold-out runs in 2001, [Interpol](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md) effectively introduced *Turn on the Bright Lights* there, and [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) graduated from the [Mercury Lounge](mercury-lounge.md) to the Bowery in 2002–2003. Later in the decade, [LCD Soundsystem](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) and [Vampire Weekend](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md) used the room for breakthrough shows.

## Notable performances / residencies

- [The Strokes](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md) — multi-night sold-out runs in 2001 around *Is This It*.
- [Interpol](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md) — 2002 release shows for *Turn on the Bright Lights*.
- [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) — 2003 shows around *Fever to Tell*.
- [LCD Soundsystem](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) — multiple sold-out nights, mid-2000s.
- [Vampire Weekend](../../../genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md) — 2008 debut-album breakthrough shows.
- Adele — early 2008 US showcase before *19* went global.
- [Patti Smith](../../../genres/punk-rock/artists/patti-smith.md) — annual benefit shows in the late 2000s and 2010s.

## Today

The Bowery Ballroom is open and operating in 2026, still under the Bowery Presents banner (now part of AEG). It is part of the Bowery Historic District but the building itself is not individually landmarked. It continues to anchor the Lower East Side venue ecosystem alongside the [Mercury Lounge](mercury-lounge.md) and the now-closed [CBGB](cbgb.md) site nearby.
