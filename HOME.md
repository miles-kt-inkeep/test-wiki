# Manhattan Music History (1970s–Present)

A wiki tracing the music made on the island of Manhattan from the early 1970s through today — the scenes that were born here, the rooms where they happened, the artists who shaped them, and the neighborhoods that gave each scene its address.

> **Sourcing note.** This wiki is built from widely-documented music history (the canon of NYC music coverage in books, magazines, and Wikipedia). Each article ends with a "Further reading" section pointing to authoritative external coverage. Where a specific claim is contested or uncertain it is marked inline `(TODO: needs source)`.

## Eras at a glance

### Early 1970s — the comedown

The [Velvet Underground](./genres/punk-rock/artists/new-york-dolls.md) had broken up, [Greenwich Village](./neighborhoods/greenwich-village/overview.md) folk had aged into singer-songwriterism, and the city was bankrupt. Two reactions formed: glam-trash rock at [Max's Kansas City](./neighborhoods/greenwich-village/venues/maxs-kansas-city.md) (the [New York Dolls](./genres/punk-rock/artists/new-york-dolls.md)) and a stripped-down rock-of-amateurs scene at a Bowery dive called [CBGB](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/cbgb.md).

### Mid–late 1970s — punk, no wave, disco, hip-hop's birth

[Punk rock](./genres/punk-rock/overview.md) coalesced at [CBGB](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/cbgb.md) around the [Ramones](./genres/punk-rock/artists/ramones.md), [Television](./genres/punk-rock/artists/television.md), [Patti Smith](./genres/punk-rock/artists/patti-smith.md), [Blondie](./genres/punk-rock/artists/blondie.md), and [Talking Heads](./genres/punk-rock/artists/talking-heads.md). Downtown art-rock turned into [no wave](./genres/no-wave/overview.md). Uptown and downtown both danced: [disco](./genres/disco/overview.md) ruled at [Studio 54](./neighborhoods/midtown-times-square/venues/studio-54.md) and the [Paradise Garage](./neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md). [Hip-hop](./genres/hip-hop/overview.md) was born in the Bronx and pulled into Manhattan within a few years.

### 1980s — clubs, cocaine, art-rock, rap crossover

[Mudd Club](./neighborhoods/soho/venues/mudd-club.md) and [Danceteria](./neighborhoods/chelsea-meatpacking/venues/danceteria.md) blended punk, art, hip-hop, and dance. [Madonna](./genres/electronic-dance/artists/madonna.md) used Danceteria as a launching pad. [Sonic Youth](./genres/no-wave/artists/sonic-youth.md) emerged from the no-wave hangover. The [Beastie Boys](./genres/hip-hop/artists/beastie-boys.md) crossed from hardcore to rap. [Hip-hop](./genres/hip-hop/overview.md) went mainstream. Real estate began rewriting the map.

### 1990s — alt-rock, Lollapalooza-era indie, the rent goes up

The [Knitting Factory](./neighborhoods/soho/venues/knitting-factory.md) and a maturing [Mercury Lounge](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) hosted indie rock and downtown jazz. The [Lower East Side](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md) became the city's de-facto rock neighborhood. Hip-hop's golden era ran in parallel uptown and across the river.

### 2000s — post-punk revival

[The Strokes](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md), [Interpol](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md), [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), [LCD Soundsystem](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md), and TV on the Radio brought a [post-punk revival](./genres/post-punk-revival/overview.md) out of [Lower East Side](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md) bars and Brooklyn lofts. The [Bowery Ballroom](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) and [Mercury Lounge](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) were the proving grounds.

### 2010s–today — diffusion

Manhattan's role shifts. [Vampire Weekend](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md) carries the indie torch out of Columbia. [A$AP Mob](./genres/hip-hop/artists/asap-mob.md) anchors a new [Harlem](./neighborhoods/harlem/overview.md) hip-hop wave. Most rooms close; some (CBGB) become retail, others (Mercury Lounge, Bowery Ballroom, Blue Note, Village Vanguard) keep going. The center of gravity drifts to Brooklyn but the island still sets the historical clock.
