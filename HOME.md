# NYC Music History (1970s–Present)

A wiki tracing the music made in New York City from the early 1970s through today — the scenes that were born here, the rooms where they happened, the artists who shaped them, and the neighborhoods that gave each scene its address. Three boroughs do most of the work in this wiki's frame: **Manhattan** held the historical center of gravity through the 1990s and remains the industry's office address; **Brooklyn** has held it residentially since 2000; **Queens** held the residential center of mainstream hip-hop from roughly 1984 through the late 1990s and is now the city's primary outdoor-concert and warehouse-dance extension.

> **Sourcing note.** This wiki is built from widely-documented music history (the canon of NYC music coverage in books, magazines, and Wikipedia). Each article ends with a "Further reading" section pointing to authoritative external coverage. Where a specific claim is contested or uncertain it is marked inline `(TODO: needs source)`.

## Eras at a glance

### Early 1970s — the comedown

The [Velvet Underground](./genres/punk-rock/artists/new-york-dolls.md) had broken up, [Greenwich Village](./neighborhoods/greenwich-village/overview.md) folk had aged into singer-songwriterism, and the city was bankrupt. Two reactions formed in Manhattan: glam-trash rock at [Max's Kansas City](./neighborhoods/greenwich-village/venues/maxs-kansas-city.md) (the [New York Dolls](./genres/punk-rock/artists/new-york-dolls.md)) and a stripped-down rock-of-amateurs scene at a Bowery dive called [CBGB](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/cbgb.md). Brooklyn at this point was almost entirely off the music map; Queens's role was residential (Louis Armstrong died at his [Corona](./neighborhoods/corona/overview.md) house in July 1971; the future [Ramones](./genres/punk-rock/artists/ramones.md) were just leaving [Forest Hills](./neighborhoods/forest-hills/overview.md) High School).

### Mid–late 1970s — punk, no wave, disco, hip-hop's birth

[Punk rock](./genres/punk-rock/overview.md) coalesced at [CBGB](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/cbgb.md) around the [Ramones](./genres/punk-rock/artists/ramones.md) (four [Forest Hills](./neighborhoods/forest-hills/overview.md), Queens kids who took the F train into Manhattan), [Television](./genres/punk-rock/artists/television.md), [Patti Smith](./genres/punk-rock/artists/patti-smith.md), [Blondie](./genres/punk-rock/artists/blondie.md), and [Talking Heads](./genres/punk-rock/artists/talking-heads.md). Downtown art-rock turned into [no wave](./genres/no-wave/overview.md). Uptown and downtown both danced: [disco](./genres/disco/overview.md) ruled at [Studio 54](./neighborhoods/midtown-times-square/venues/studio-54.md) and the [Paradise Garage](./neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md). [Hip-hop](./genres/hip-hop/overview.md) was born in the Bronx and pulled into Manhattan within a few years.

### 1980s — clubs, cocaine, art-rock, rap crossover, Queens hip-hop arrives

[Mudd Club](./neighborhoods/soho/venues/mudd-club.md) and [Danceteria](./neighborhoods/chelsea-meatpacking/venues/danceteria.md) blended punk, art, hip-hop, and dance. [Madonna](./genres/electronic-dance/artists/madonna.md) used Danceteria as a launching pad. [Sonic Youth](./genres/no-wave/artists/sonic-youth.md) emerged from the no-wave hangover. The [Beastie Boys](./genres/hip-hop/artists/beastie-boys.md) crossed from hardcore to rap. [Hip-hop](./genres/hip-hop/overview.md) went mainstream — and the residential half of it came out of Queens. [Run-DMC](./genres/hip-hop/artists/run-dmc.md) (from [Hollis](./neighborhoods/hollis/overview.md), founded 1981) and [LL Cool J](./genres/hip-hop/artists/ll-cool-j.md) (Hollis, signed to Def Jam in 1984) anchored Def Jam's commercial breakthrough; Marley Marl and MC Shan put the [Queensbridge Houses](./neighborhoods/long-island-city/overview.md) on the map through the 1986 Bridge Wars. Across the river, [Bedford-Stuyvesant](./neighborhoods/bedford-stuyvesant/overview.md) and the Marcy Houses in southern [Williamsburg](./neighborhoods/williamsburg/overview.md) were producing the next generation of MCs — work that would surface as [The Notorious B.I.G.](./genres/hip-hop/artists/notorious-big.md) and [Jay-Z](./genres/hip-hop/artists/jay-z.md) by the mid-1990s. Real estate began rewriting the map.

### 1990s — alt-rock, hip-hop's golden era, Queensbridge and Brooklyn peak

The [Knitting Factory](./neighborhoods/soho/venues/knitting-factory.md) and a maturing [Mercury Lounge](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) hosted indie rock and downtown jazz. The [Lower East Side](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md) became the city's de-facto rock neighborhood. In Brooklyn, hip-hop's golden age ran in parallel — Biggie's *Ready to Die* (1994) and Jay-Z's *Reasonable Doubt* (1996) put [Bedford-Stuyvesant](./neighborhoods/bedford-stuyvesant/overview.md) and the Marcy Projects on the global rap map. Across the East River in Queens, [Nas](./genres/hip-hop/artists/nas.md)'s *Illmatic* (1994) and [Mobb Deep](./genres/hip-hop/artists/mobb-deep.md)'s *The Infamous* (1995) — both Queensbridge records released within twelve months of each other — made the [Queensbridge Houses](./neighborhoods/long-island-city/overview.md) the most-documented single building complex in rap history. [A Tribe Called Quest](./genres/hip-hop/artists/tribe-called-quest.md) anchored the Native Tongues alternative-rap canon out of [St. Albans / Linden Boulevard](./neighborhoods/jamaica/overview.md) on the [Jamaica](./neighborhoods/jamaica/overview.md) side of Queens. Williamsburg's first warehouse parties and rooftop shows began drifting north from a still-mostly-industrial waterfront.

### 2000s — post-punk revival, Brooklyn arrives, Queens commercializes

[The Strokes](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/strokes.md), [Interpol](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md), [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), [LCD Soundsystem](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md), and [TV on the Radio](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/tv-on-the-radio.md) brought a [post-punk revival](./genres/post-punk-revival/overview.md) out of [Lower East Side](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md) bars *and* Brooklyn lofts. The [Bowery Ballroom](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) and [Mercury Lounge](./neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) were the proving grounds; by 2008 [Grizzly Bear](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/grizzly-bear.md), [Dirty Projectors](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/dirty-projectors.md), and the rest of the Brooklyn indie wave had relocated the conversation to the East River's far bank. In Queens, [South Jamaica](./neighborhoods/jamaica/overview.md) produced the decade's biggest single rap commercial success in 50 Cent (*Get Rich or Die Tryin'*, 2003); Ja Rule's parallel career out of [Hollis](./neighborhoods/hollis/overview.md) carried the borough's institutional rap presence through the early 2000s.

### 2010s–today — Brooklyn dominant, Queens diversifies, Manhattan as office

Manhattan's role shifts. [Vampire Weekend](./genres/post-punk-revival/artists/vampire-weekend.md) carries the indie torch out of Columbia. [A$AP Mob](./genres/hip-hop/artists/asap-mob.md) anchors a new [Harlem](./neighborhoods/harlem/overview.md) hip-hop wave. Most Manhattan rooms close; some (CBGB) become retail, others (Mercury Lounge, Bowery Ballroom, Blue Note, Village Vanguard) keep going. Brooklyn becomes the city's actual music neighborhood — [Brooklyn Steel](./neighborhoods/bushwick/venues/brooklyn-steel.md), [Music Hall of Williamsburg](./neighborhoods/williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md), [Saint Vitus](./neighborhoods/greenpoint/venues/saint-vitus.md), and a long tail of warehouses in Bushwick, East Williamsburg, and [Ridgewood](./neighborhoods/ridgewood/overview.md) take the live-music load. The Queens-side warehouse extension — [Knockdown Center](./neighborhoods/ridgewood/venues/knockdown-center.md) (2014), [Nowadays](./neighborhoods/ridgewood/venues/nowadays.md) (2015), and [Trans-Pecos](./neighborhoods/ridgewood/venues/trans-pecos.md) (2014) — now carries roughly half the city's underground-dance circuit; [Forest Hills Stadium](./neighborhoods/forest-hills/venues/forest-hills-stadium.md), renovated and reopened in 2013, is the city's largest outdoor seasonal concert venue. Brooklyn drill emerges out of [Bedford-Stuyvesant](./neighborhoods/bedford-stuyvesant/overview.md) and Canarsie around 2019, briefly making Pop Smoke the most-watched young rapper in America before his death. Nicki Minaj — raised in [Jamaica](./neighborhoods/jamaica/overview.md) and [Long Island City](./neighborhoods/long-island-city/overview.md) — anchors the female-rap revolution of the 2010s. Manhattan still sets the historical clock; Brooklyn now sets the present-day one; Queens sets the residential hip-hop one across both decades.
