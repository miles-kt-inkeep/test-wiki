# Holy Ghost!

Holy Ghost! is the New York dance duo of Alex Frankel and Nick Millhiser, childhood friends from the Upper West Side who signed to DFA Records in 2007 and became the label's most-pop-leaning late-2000s signing. Their catalog updates the synth-pop and Italo-disco end of the DFA template that [LCD Soundsystem](../../post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) and [The Juan MacLean](juan-maclean.md) had already laid down, with songwriting that leans more toward verse-chorus pop than club-track form. They sit in the [electronic dance overview](../overview.md) lineage as the DFA roster's pure dance-pop voice of the early 2010s.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Frankel and Millhiser grew up together in Manhattan, attended LaGuardia High School, and first worked together in the early-2000s hip-hop production duo Automato — a project that briefly orbited DFA before dissolving. They re-formed as Holy Ghost! in the mid-2000s, signed to DFA in 2007, and broke through with the 2007 12-inch "Hold On" and the 2008 single "Hold My Breath." A long string of EPs and remixes for DFA labelmates followed before the full-length debut.

## Sound and signature work

*Holy Ghost!* (DFA, 2011) is the debut LP — "Wait & See," "Hold On," and "Hudson Heights" run a tight synth-pop / Italo template, with cameos from Michael McDonald and others. *Dynamics* (DFA, 2013) and the *Crime Cutz* EP (West End / DFA, 2016) followed. Subsequent EPs including *Work* (2019) have continued the project on an irregular release schedule. Alongside the duo's own records, Millhiser and Frankel have produced and remixed widely for the DFA roster and beyond.

## Manhattan venue history

- DFA Records, West 13th Street — production base for the catalog.
- Le Bain, the Standard High Line — Manhattan DJ residencies and showcases.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) and Webster Hall — peak-era headlining shows.
- Output (Williamsburg, 2013–2018) — recurring Brooklyn extension bookings.

## Legacy

Holy Ghost! is the DFA roster's clearest answer to the question of what a New York dance-pop duo sounds like in the [LCD Soundsystem](../../post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) era. They are also one of the few acts on this list who actually grew up in Manhattan, which gives the catalog a different relationship to the city than the various transplants who built DFA around them.
