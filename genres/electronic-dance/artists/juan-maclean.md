# The Juan MacLean

The Juan MacLean is the recording and performing alias of John MacLean, a New Hampshire-raised producer and DJ who became one of DFA Records' founding artists and James Murphy's longest-running collaborator. Where [LCD Soundsystem](../../post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) and the [Rapture](../../post-punk-revival/artists/rapture.md) approached dance music through a post-punk lens, MacLean has been DFA's most consistently dance-floor-facing solo voice — a producer making records that read as Manhattan-routed updates of Detroit techno and Chicago house in the [Frankie Knuckles](frankie-knuckles.md) lineage that the [electronic dance overview](../overview.md) traces.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Before DFA, MacLean played guitar in the abrasive post-hardcore band Six Finger Satellite — the same Sub Pop-adjacent group James Murphy produced and toured with in the late 1990s — and that working relationship became the seed of DFA. When Murphy and Tim Goldsworthy founded the label on West 13th Street in 2001, MacLean was among the first artists they signed, with the 2002 single "By the Time I Get to Venus" running on the label's catalog from its earliest days. Subsequent singles, including "You Can't Have It Both Ways" (2003) and "Tito's Way" (2004), established his analog-synth-and-drum-machine aesthetic.

## Sound and signature work

*Less Than Human* (DFA, 2005) was the debut album, leaning toward live-band electro and Devo / Liaisons Dangereuses references. *The Future Will Come* (DFA, 2009) is widely considered the peak — a sequence of co-vocal records with Nancy Whang of LCD Soundsystem ("Happy House," "The Simple Life") that pulled the project toward proper New York house. *In a Dream* (DFA, 2014) continued the Whang collaboration, with "A Place Called Space" becoming a club staple. The 2017 12-inch "The Brighter the Light," again with Whang, was an Album of the Week pick across the dance press. Throughout, MacLean has worked as a touring DJ on the international club and festival circuit and has remixed extensively for DFA labelmates.

## Manhattan venue history

- DFA Records, West 13th Street — production base for the entire catalog.
- Plant Bar, East Village — early-2000s DFA-adjacent DJ residency.
- Le Bain, the Standard High Line — Manhattan DJ residencies.
- Cielo and Output (Williamsburg) — peak-era club bookings on either side of the river.

## Legacy

The Juan MacLean is the DFA artist whose work most directly continues the New York house lineage. The Murphy / MacLean working relationship — production partner, label co-founder, vocal collaborator — is one of the spine pieces of DFA's whole 2000s-2010s output, and the Whang vocal records have become the project's most-cited entry point. The catalog is the closest thing DFA has to a sustained, dance-first solo discography.
