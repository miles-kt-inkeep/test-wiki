# Hercules & Love Affair

Hercules & Love Affair is the rotating disco-house project of producer and DJ Andy Butler, founded in New York and released on DFA Records starting in 2007. The project's 2008 self-titled debut — anchored by the single "Blind," with lead vocals by [Antony Hegarty](https://www.theguardian.com/music/2008/mar/14/electronicmusic.popandrock1) (later Anohni) — translated [Larry Levan](../../disco/artists/larry-levan.md)'s [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) and Salsoul-era disco into a contemporary, queer-forward Manhattan sound that explicitly inherited the lineage the [electronic dance overview](../overview.md) traces.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Butler grew up in Denver and moved to New York in the early 2000s, where he immersed himself in the post-Garage [Body & Soul](francois-k.md) Sunday-afternoon party at Vinyl on Hudson Street and the wider downtown queer dance underground. He began building a rotating live ensemble around himself — Anohni was an early collaborator from the city's downtown art-pop scene — and DFA signed the project in 2007 on the strength of demos that read as deliberate Salsoul / [Frankie Knuckles](frankie-knuckles.md) homage. The label was a precise fit: James Murphy and Tim Goldsworthy had built DFA around the same lineage that Butler was openly trying to extend.

## Sound and signature work

*Hercules and Love Affair* (DFA / Mute, 2008) is the canonical text. "Blind" — a slow-burn disco production with Anohni's countertenor over a French-horn arrangement — was a runaway hit, charting in the UK and becoming one of DFA's most-recognized singles outside [LCD Soundsystem](../../post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md). The follow-ups *Blue Songs* (2011), *The Feast of the Broken Heart* (2014), and *Omnion* (2017) shifted toward harder house and acid, with Butler eventually relocating to Europe and rotating in vocalists including Kim Ann Foxman, Nomi, Aerea Negrot, and Sharon Van Etten. *In Amber* (2022) was the most-recent release as of this writing.

## Manhattan venue history

- Body & Soul / Vinyl, Hudson Street — Butler's formative dance-floor education.
- DFA Records, West 13th Street — production base for the 2008 debut.
- Le Bain, the Standard High Line — repeated Manhattan DJ residencies and showcases.
- Terminal 5 and Webster Hall — peak-era live appearances.

## Legacy

Hercules & Love Affair is the DFA-era act that made the disco-house lineage explicit. Where [LCD Soundsystem](../../post-punk-revival/artists/lcd-soundsystem.md) and the [Rapture](../../post-punk-revival/artists/rapture.md) approached dance music from the post-punk side, Butler approached it as a Paradise Garage believer, and the project's queer-forward aesthetic reconnected DFA explicitly to the [Mancuso](david-mancuso.md) / [Levan](../../disco/artists/larry-levan.md) tradition that birthed the genre. Antony Hegarty's transition to Anohni and continued collaborations have kept "Blind" in regular rotation.
