# François K

François Kevorkian (born 1954, Rodez, France), known professionally as François K, is a producer, engineer, mix engineer, and DJ who has been at the center of Manhattan dance music for 45 years. He arrived in New York from Paris in 1975, came up as a drummer at the Galaxy 21 (where he played live to [Walter Gibbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Gibbons)' DJ sets), and then built a career that spans two distinct contributions to the genre: a 1980s mix-and-engineer catalog at Prelude Records, Sigma Sound, and his own Axis Studios, and a 1990s–2010s DJ residency at the Body & Soul Sunday-afternoon party at Vinyl on Hudson Street that the [electronic dance overview](../overview.md) treats as the explicit successor to David Mancuso's Loft and [Larry Levan](../../disco/artists/larry-levan.md)'s [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md).

## Formation and Manhattan context

Kevorkian moved to New York at 21 to study music. His first paid work in Manhattan was as the live drummer accompanying Walter Gibbons's DJ sets at Galaxy 21 — an arrangement that gave him a working education in disco programming and arrangement from inside the booth. By 1978 he was the head A&R and in-house remixer at Prelude Records (the home of D Train and Musique), and he became one of the small handful of mixers — alongside Tom Moulton, Larry Levan, and Tee Scott — credited with defining the 12-inch-single form.

His circle through the 1980s and 1990s included Larry Levan (whom he toured Japan with in the legendary 1992 "Harmony Tour" the year of Levan's death), [Frankie Knuckles](./frankie-knuckles.md), Danny Krivit, Joe Claussell, and Body & Soul co-residents at Vinyl on Hudson Street. He has run his own Wave Music label and Axis Studios in Manhattan — first on West 18th Street, then from a Greenwich Street location — for the last three decades.

## Sound and signature work

Kevorkian's mix and production credits are a working tour of post-disco club music: D Train's "You're the One for Me" (1981), Yazoo's "Situation" (1982 NYC remix), Smokey Robinson's "And I Don't Love You" (1981, with Larry Levan), Depeche Mode's "People Are People" (1984 remix), Diana Ross's "Love Hangover" (1985 remix), Kraftwerk's "Tour de France" (1983 remix). His mixes are characterized by dub-influenced delays, exaggerated low-end, and arrangements that explicitly reference Jamaican mixing engineers like [King Tubby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Tubby) and [Lee "Scratch" Perry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_%22Scratch%22_Perry) — a dub-disco fusion he is widely credited with formalizing.

His DJ residency at Body & Soul (Sunday afternoons at Vinyl on 6 Hubert Street, then at various Manhattan and Brooklyn locations after Vinyl's 2002 closure) ran with co-residents Danny Krivit and Joe Claussell from 1996 onward. Body & Soul was explicitly a Loft / Garage continuation: alcohol-light, racially and sexually mixed, audiophile-leaning, gospel-vocal-heavy, and built around extended mixing rather than peak-time spectacle.

## Manhattan venue history

- Galaxy 21 (1975–1977) — the live-drum-and-DJ apprenticeship with Walter Gibbons.
- Prelude Records, 200 West 57th Street (1978–1985) — head of A&R and in-house remixer.
- Body & Soul at Vinyl, 6 Hubert Street (1996–2002) — the Sunday-afternoon residency with Danny Krivit and Joe Claussell. Vinyl closed in 2002 after a fire; the party continued at Club Shelter, Cielo, and Brooklyn locations.
- Cielo, 18 Little West 12th Street — Body & Soul Sundays from approximately 2003.
- Axis Studios — Manhattan production and mixing studio in operation since the 1990s.
- Deep Space at Cielo (Monday nights, 2004 onward) — Kevorkian's solo dub-electronic residency, the longest-running Manhattan club residency of the 21st century.

## Legacy

Kevorkian remains an active touring DJ, label boss (Wave Music), and producer. The combined résumé — a foundational 1980s remix catalog plus a continuing residency from the 1990s into the 2020s — makes him the most institutionally durable working figure in Manhattan dance music after the Loft / Garage generation. The [electronic dance](../overview.md) overview's framing of the Body & Soul party as the formal Loft / Garage continuation rests primarily on his and Krivit's residency there. The Deep Space party at Cielo, which ran for over a decade, is widely cited as the longest-running 21st-century Manhattan dance residency.
