# Frankie Knuckles

Frankie Knuckles (born Francis Nicholls, 1955–2014) is universally credited as the "Godfather of House" — the genre takes its name from his Chicago residency at the Warehouse (1977–1982). But his formative decade was Manhattan: born in the South Bronx, he came up alongside [Larry Levan](../../disco/artists/larry-levan.md) at the Continental Baths and Better Days in the early-to-mid 1970s, learning the trade in the same Manhattan dance underground that produced David Mancuso, Nicky Siano, and the [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) sound. The Manhattan-to-Chicago move in 1977 — and the records he made and remixed once he got there — drew the line that separates the Manhattan "garage" lineage from the Chicago "house" lineage that grew out of it.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Knuckles met [Larry Levan](../../disco/artists/larry-levan.md) as a teenager at the Manhattan branch of Charles Gallery (a uniform store on 125th Street where both worked); the friendship lasted until Levan's death in 1992. Both came up under Nicky Siano at the Gallery in [SoHo](../../../neighborhoods/soho/overview.md), assisting with lights, drugs, and sound. Their first joint residency was the Continental Baths — the gay bathhouse at the Ansonia Hotel on West 73rd Street where the floor show ran from roughly 1971 through 1974 and where both men taught themselves to DJ on a single Thorens turntable.

When the Baths closed for a 1974 renovation, Knuckles and Levan went their separate ways: Levan stayed in Manhattan and eventually took the [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) gig in 1977; Knuckles took a residency at SoHo Place and Better Days before [Robert Williams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Williams_(promoter)) — a US Studio promoter who had come up in the Mancuso / Loft orbit — flew him to Chicago in 1977 to inaugurate the Warehouse.

## Sound and signature work

Knuckles's Continental Baths and Better Days sets were post-Loft, post-Gallery extensions of Mancuso's audiophile model — extended disco edits, gospel vocals, deep low-end. What he built in Chicago at the Warehouse (and afterwards at Power Plant, 1983–1987) was the same sensibility scaled into a working-class Black-and-Latino setting: longer mixes, a heavier reliance on his own reel-to-reel edits, and eventually his own productions made on Roland TR-909 / TB-303 with Jamie Principle and others. Records like "Your Love" (1985) and "Tears" (1989, with Robert Owens) are the hinge between Manhattan disco/garage and Chicago house.

Knuckles returned to New York in 1987, by which point both the Warehouse and the [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) had closed. He took the residency at the Sound Factory Bar in Manhattan and built a parallel remix career — Pet Shop Boys' "I Want a Dog" (1988), Lisa Stansfield's "Change" (1991), Diana Ross's "I'm Coming Out (1994 remix)," Michael Jackson's "Rock with You (1997 remix)" — that won the first Grammy for Remixer of the Year in 1997.

## Manhattan venue history

- The Continental Baths, 230 West 74th Street (1972–1974) — the apprenticeship room with [Larry Levan](../../disco/artists/larry-levan.md).
- Better Days, 316 West 49th Street (1974–1977) — the Midtown gay club residency before Chicago.
- SoHo Place — short late-1976 / early-1977 residency.
- Sound Factory Bar (1987 onward, intermittent through the 1990s) — the post-Chicago Manhattan return.
- The Roxy and Body & Soul guest spots in the 1990s and 2000s.

## Legacy

Knuckles is the link between the Manhattan dance underground and the genre that became the global default of the next forty years. After 1987 he was based in Chicago and New York simultaneously, and was widely regarded as the emotional through-line of the Loft / Garage / Warehouse continuum until his death in March 2014 from complications of type 2 diabetes. His Manhattan years are why this article sits in the [electronic dance](../overview.md) section and not just in a Chicago-house article — the [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) and the Warehouse share parents, and Knuckles is one of them.
