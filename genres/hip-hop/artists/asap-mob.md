# A$AP Mob

A$AP Mob is a [hip-hop](../overview.md) collective founded in [Harlem](../../../neighborhoods/harlem/overview.md), Manhattan, around 2007 by Steven "A$AP Yams" Rodriguez alongside A$AP Bari and A$AP Illz. The crew rose to mainstream attention in 2011 through breakout member A$AP Rocky and his viral mixtape *LiveLoveA$AP* (2011), followed by his major-label debut *Long.Live.A$AP* (2013). At its peak the Mob included A$AP Rocky, A$AP Ferg, A$AP Nast, A$AP Twelvyy, A$AP Ant, and producers including A$AP Lou and Hector Delgado, with A$AP Yams as the curatorial brain. Yams's 2015 death cast a long shadow over the collective's subsequent work.

## Formation and Manhattan context

A$AP Yams grew up in Harlem and assembled the collective as a fashion-and-music brotherhood with origins in the housing projects around 145th Street. The "A$AP" prefix denoted "Always Strive and Prosper," and the crew's earliest activity was as much about Tumblr-aesthetic curation — Three 6 Mafia / Memphis chopped-and-screwed influences, '90s designer-fashion homage — as it was about rapping.

A$AP Rocky (Rakim Mayers) was the first commercial breakout when his "Peso" video racked up YouTube millions in 2011. He was signed to RCA / Sony for a then-record $3 million deal, and the Mob's center of gravity stayed firmly in [Harlem](../../../neighborhoods/harlem/overview.md) and the wider [hip-hop](../overview.md) New York ecosystem, even as members traveled constantly.

## Sound and signature work

A$AP Rocky's *LiveLoveA$AP* mixtape (October 2011) established the template — Houston-influenced screwed beats, Memphis-rap reference, designer-label name-dropping in a distinctly post-internet New York register. *Long.Live.A$AP* (January 2013) debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and produced "F\*\*kin' Problems" with Drake, 2 Chainz, and Kendrick Lamar. *At.Long.Last.A$AP* (2015), released after Yams's death and dedicated to him, leaned psychedelic and is widely regarded as Rocky's best.

The compilation album *Cozy Tapes Vol. 1: Friends* (2016) and *Vol. 2: Too Cozy* (2017) were full Mob projects. A$AP Ferg's *Trap Lord* (2013) and *Always Strive and Prosper* (2016) gave him a parallel solo career. Rocky's *Testing* (2018) and the long-anticipated *Don't Be Dumb* (2024/2025) have continued his arc.

## Manhattan venue history

- [Apollo Theater](../../../neighborhoods/harlem/venues/apollo-theater.md) — A$AP Rocky and Mob shows celebrating their Harlem origins.
- Hammerstein Ballroom — multiple Mob-era headlining shows.
- Terminal 5 — early-career New York runs.
- Madison Square Garden — sold-out arena shows for Rocky from the mid-2010s onward.
- Yams Day at Barclays Center — annual memorial concert founded after Yams's 2015 death.

## Legacy

A$AP Yams died on January 18, 2015, of an accidental drug overdose at age 26 — a foundational loss for the crew and a significant moment in 2010s [hip-hop](../overview.md). The annual Yams Day concerts have featured most members of the Mob plus collaborators ranging from Tyler, the Creator to Playboi Carti.

The Mob's impact on [Harlem](../../../neighborhoods/harlem/overview.md) and on New York rap is enormous: they were the first East Coast crew to fully metabolize Southern rap and internet-fashion culture, opening the door for later New York acts in the Lil Uzi Vert / Playboi Carti / Sheck Wes orbit. A$AP Rocky's relationship with Rihanna and his role as a fashion-world fixture (Vivienne Westwood, Gucci, his AWGE creative agency) have made him one of the most globally recognized New York rappers of his generation.
