# LL Cool J

LL Cool J (born James Todd Smith, January 14, 1968) is a rapper from [Hollis](../../../neighborhoods/hollis/overview.md), Queens, whose 1985 Def Jam debut *Radio* made him the first solo rap star and made Def Jam Recordings — Rick Rubin and Russell Simmons's then-eight-month-old label — into a viable commercial business. Across 13 studio albums from 1985 to 2013, LL Cool J is the longest continuously-active commercial career in rap history; he was the first rapper inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a primary artist (2021, with the Musical Excellence Award), and his Hollis residential biography is one of the wiki's cleanest hip-hop-as-residential-Queens stories.

## Origins and Hollis geography

James Todd Smith was born in Bay Shore, Long Island, to Ondrea Smith and James Nunya Smith. His parents separated when he was four; he moved with his mother to her parents' house at 191-44 Murdock Avenue in [Hollis](../../../neighborhoods/hollis/overview.md), Queens, around 1972. The Murdock Avenue house — his maternal grandparents Eugene and Ellen Griffith's home — was where he wrote and recorded his earliest demos and where he lived through most of his teens. The Griffiths bought their grandson his first DJ equipment and a four-track tape recorder when he was twelve; by fourteen Smith was demoing in the basement and mailing tapes to local labels and DJs.

The Murdock Avenue address sits about five blocks south of the [Run-DMC](./run-dmc.md) houses around 205th Street and Hollis Avenue — close enough that the teenage LL knew Run, DMC, and Jay socially through the neighborhood, but a generation behind them in age (Smith was three to four years younger than the Run-DMC members). The age gap meant he watched Run-DMC's first commercial success in 1983–1984 as a high-schooler rather than as a peer; his early demos were partly modeled on the Run-DMC template.

## The Def Jam signing (1984)

In 1984 Smith mailed a four-track demo tape to Def Jam's then-new address at 5 University Place in Manhattan (Rick Rubin's NYU dorm room). Rubin and Simmons signed him a few weeks later — Smith was sixteen years old. His first single, "I Need a Beat" (Def Jam, November 1984), was the label's first commercially-released single and sold 100,000 copies as an independent 12-inch.

## Key work

### *Radio* (Def Jam, November 1985)

The debut. *Radio* was Def Jam's first full-length album release and the second Def Jam recording overall (after various single 12-inches). Produced by Rick Rubin, the album's sparse drum-machine-and-808 production — even sparser than [Run-DMC](./run-dmc.md)'s contemporary work — gave LL's lyrical performance the entire foreground. "I Can't Live Without My Radio" became a chart single and the album's signature track; "Rock the Bells," "I Can Give You More," and "I Need a Beat" filled out the album. *Radio* went platinum in 1986, making LL Cool J the first solo rap artist with a platinum-selling debut.

### *Bigger and Deffer* (Def Jam, May 1987)

The second album. "I Need Love" — a slow, sentimental rap ballad with Smith rhyming over a synth-pad melody — was one of the first rap singles to cross over to soft-rock radio formats; the song reached #14 on the Billboard Hot 100 and #1 on the R&B chart. The album sold three million copies and made LL Cool J the first rap solo artist with a multi-platinum follow-up.

### *Mama Said Knock You Out* (Def Jam, August 1990)

The commercial comeback after the lukewarm 1989 *Walking with a Panther*. Produced by Marley Marl (from [Queensbridge](../../../neighborhoods/long-island-city/overview.md) and the Juice Crew), the title track restored LL's "tough" rap reputation; "Around the Way Girl" became his biggest Hot 100 single. The album won the Grammy for Best Rap Solo Performance in 1992.

### Acting career

From 1995 LL Cool J began a parallel acting career — *In the House* (NBC, 1995–1999), *Any Given Sunday* (1999), the *NCIS: Los Angeles* series (CBS, 2009–2023, fourteen seasons) — that ultimately surpassed his music career in cultural visibility. He is, by some measure, the rap artist who most successfully made the transition into mainstream-actor work; his post-2000 music output continued (eight studio albums between 1995 and 2013) but his cultural presence shifted toward television.

### *Authentic* (Def Jam, 2013)

The thirteenth studio album, and the most recent at this writing. The album included guest appearances from Eddie Van Halen, Travis Barker, Charlie Wilson, and Brad Paisley; the reception was mixed but the album peaked at #3 on the Billboard 200. After *Authentic* LL Cool J released no further studio albums through 2026 (a long-promised *Force* album has been delayed multiple times).

## NYC venue history

LL Cool J's earliest performances were Hollis house parties and Queens park shows. The 1985 *Radio* tour played the [Latin Quarter](../overview.md#origins) (96 Lexington Avenue, Manhattan), the [Apollo Theater](../../../neighborhoods/harlem/venues/apollo-theater.md), and small-room dates throughout the city before the album's platinum-certification arena bookings. The 1986 Def Jam tour (with Run-DMC, the Beastie Boys, and Whodini) played Madison Square Garden in November 1986 as part of the *Raising Hell* run. Through the 1990s and 2000s LL continued to appear at the Apollo, Madison Square Garden, and various tribute and benefit events; his post-2010 New York performances have been concentrated on televised events and Grammy / Rock Hall ceremonies rather than touring dates.

## Legacy

Within the per-borough hip-hop frame, LL Cool J anchors the [Hollis](../../../neighborhoods/hollis/overview.md) / [Queens](../../../neighborhoods/queens.md) commercial-rap canon alongside [Run-DMC](./run-dmc.md). Within the broader genre frame, he is the first rap solo artist with a sustained 1980s-to-2010s commercial career — every solo rap career that has run more than three decades (Jay-Z, Snoop Dogg, Eminem) has cited LL as the precedent. His 2021 Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction (Musical Excellence Award, not the standard Performer category) acknowledged the cross-over career; his ongoing cultural visibility through *NCIS: Los Angeles* extended his name recognition past the music-only frame in which most of his peers stayed.
