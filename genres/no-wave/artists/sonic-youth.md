# Sonic Youth

Sonic Youth was an American rock band formed in Manhattan in 1981 by Thurston Moore, Kim Gordon, and Lee Ranaldo, with Steve Shelley joining as drummer in 1985. Rooted in the [no wave](../overview.md) and post-punk scenes of the early-'80s [Lower East Side](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md), they spent thirty years systematically demolishing and rebuilding the rock-guitar idiom through alternate tunings, prepared guitars, and sheer volume. Their 1988 double album *Daydream Nation* is widely regarded as one of the defining American records of the era. They effectively ceased operating after the 2011 separation of Moore and Gordon.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Thurston Moore moved to New York from Connecticut in 1976; Kim Gordon arrived from Los Angeles in 1980. They met through the downtown art world and were married in 1984. Lee Ranaldo had played in Glenn Branca's guitar ensemble — a key training ground — and brought that maximalist approach to the new band. Their first performance was at the Noise Festival in June 1981 at White Columns gallery in [SoHo](../../../neighborhoods/soho/overview.md), an event Moore co-curated.

The early band lived and worked in [Lower East Side](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md) lofts, recording cheaply and playing the [Knitting Factory](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/knitting-factory.md), CBGB's hardcore matinees, and assorted lofts and galleries. They were of the second wave that followed [no wave](../overview.md) acts like Teenage Jesus and the Jerks and DNA, mixing that scene's atonality with hardcore-punk velocity.

## Sound and signature work

After early experimental records (*Confusion Is Sex*, 1983; *Bad Moon Rising*, 1985), they hit *EVOL* (1986) and *Sister* (1987), which pulled their noise toward something approaching pop song-craft. *Daydream Nation* (1988) is their masterpiece — a two-LP set ("Teen Age Riot," "The Sprawl," "Candle") that earned them a major-label deal with DGC.

*Goo* (1990) and *Dirty* (1992) carried them through the alternative-rock boom. *Washing Machine* (1995) and *Murray Street* (2002) showed continued growth. Their final album, *The Eternal* (2009), arrived as the band's nearly thirty-year run wound down. The signature is unmistakable: Moore and Ranaldo's drumstick-jammed, screwdriver-prepared, wildly-tuned Fender Jazzmasters and Jaguars, plus Gordon's deadpan-cool spoken-shouted vocals.

## Manhattan venue history

- [Knitting Factory](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/knitting-factory.md) — frequent residency in the late '80s and '90s.
- [CBGB](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/cbgb.md) — Sunday hardcore matinees in the early '80s.
- [Mercury Lounge](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) — secret shows in the '90s and 2000s.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — multiple late-period New York runs.
- White Columns gallery (SoHo) — debut performance at the 1981 Noise Festival.
- The Kitchen (Chelsea) — early avant-garde performances.

## Legacy

Sonic Youth's catalog opened the door for an enormous swath of American independent music — from Nirvana, whom Sonic Youth famously brought to DGC, to every subsequent noise-pop, drone, or indie-rock band in New York. Their fingerprints are obvious on [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](../../post-punk-revival/artists/yeah-yeah-yeahs.md), [Interpol](../../post-punk-revival/artists/interpol.md), and the broader early-2000s [post-punk revival](../../post-punk-revival/overview.md).

The band has not formally reunited since the 2011 separation of Moore and Gordon, though all four members remain musically active in solo projects, Body/Head (Gordon), and Chelsea Light Moving (Moore). Gordon's 2015 memoir *Girl in a Band* is a key document of [Lower East Side](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md) life.
