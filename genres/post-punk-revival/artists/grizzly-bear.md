# Grizzly Bear

Grizzly Bear is an indie-rock band based in [Williamsburg](../../../neighborhoods/williamsburg/overview.md) and [Greenpoint](../../../neighborhoods/greenpoint/overview.md), Brooklyn — though the band's official formation in 2002 began as Edward Droste's solo home-recording project — that became the late-2000s flagship for the chamber-folk and harmony-driven side of the Brooklyn indie wave. Across four studio albums between 2006 and 2017 they produced one of the most-praised catalogs to come out of post-2005 Brooklyn.

## Origins and Brooklyn geography

Edward Droste began Grizzly Bear as a solo home-recording project in his Williamsburg apartment in 2002, recording on a four-track and self-distributing the early demos that became *Horn of Plenty* (2004). The expansion to a four-piece — Daniel Rossen (guitar / vocals, joined 2005), Chris Taylor (bass / production / multi-instrumentalist), and Christopher Bear (drums) — happened as Rossen's parallel band Department of Eagles wound down. From 2006 onward all four members were Brooklyn-based, with Rossen and Bear especially closely associated with [Greenpoint](../../../neighborhoods/greenpoint/overview.md) at various points and Droste with Williamsburg / South Williamsburg.

Recording largely happened in Brooklyn (Taylor's studios, various rented loft spaces) supplemented with trips to upstate New York for the more orchestral pieces of each album. The band's identification as Brooklyn — rather than as another more anonymous indie-band-in-New-York — was central to how the music press placed them.

## Sound and signature work

Grizzly Bear's sound combined four-part vocal harmonies, finger-picked acoustic guitars, woodwinds and brass arrangements (often arranged by Taylor or by collaborators including the choirs that recur on the band's records), and unusual time signatures used unobtrusively. The result was chamber-folk by way of Brian Wilson and the *Pet Sounds* tradition, with a dynamics range — very quiet to very loud, often within the same song — that made the live show distinctive.

### *Yellow House* (Warp, September 2006)

The first full-band album. Critically launched the band; Pitchfork's coverage of *Yellow House* is the moment Grizzly Bear becomes a publicly-watched name. The album was largely recorded at Droste's mother's house in Cape Cod, a fact incorporated into the album's name and aesthetic.

### *Veckatimest* (Warp, May 2009)

The commercial breakthrough. The single "Two Weeks" was an unlikely Pitchfork-and-NPR crossover; *Veckatimest* debuted at number eight on the Billboard 200 — at the time the highest chart position any Warp Records album had achieved in the U.S. — and made the band the most-watched Brooklyn indie act of its year. The album's recording happened in part at the Allaire Studios upstate (Catskills) and in Brooklyn.

### *Shields* (Warp, September 2012)

The fall-from-peak album. *Shields* was less of a commercial step up than *Veckatimest* had been, but its structural ambition (longer songs, denser arrangements, more guitar-foreground writing from Rossen) made it the band's most musically ambitious work. The album was recorded in part in Texas and finished in Brooklyn.

### *Painted Ruins* (RCA, August 2017)

The fourth and (as of 2026) final studio album. Released after a five-year hiatus during which all four members pursued solo projects (Rossen's *Silent Hour / Golden Mile* EP and *You Belong There*; Taylor's CANT project; Droste's solo single work). *Painted Ruins* received warm if not ecstatic critical coverage; the band has been on indefinite hiatus since the 2018 tour.

## NYC venue history

The band's Brooklyn venue footprint included early Northsix, [Music Hall of Williamsburg](../../../neighborhoods/williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md) (multiple), and various loft and warehouse shows. On the Manhattan side they followed the standard ladder — Mercury Lounge / Bowery Ballroom / Webster Hall / Terminal 5 / Radio City — culminating in three-night stands at Radio City Music Hall in 2010 and 2013.

## Legacy

Grizzly Bear's primary historical importance to the wiki's frame is as the chamber-folk-and-harmony flagship of the post-2005 Brooklyn indie wave. Where [TV on the Radio](./tv-on-the-radio.md) opened the door for Brooklyn-bred indie rock in the post-punk-revival era, and [LCD Soundsystem](./lcd-soundsystem.md) carried the dance-punk side of the conversation, Grizzly Bear's contribution was specifically to push the wave toward the orchestrated, harmony-rich, slow-build sound that defined the late-2000s "Brooklyn indie" template that the press and the broader public came to recognize.
