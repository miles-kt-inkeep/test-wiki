# Dirty Projectors

Dirty Projectors is David Longstreth's polyrhythmic indie-rock ensemble — a band whose lineup has rotated repeatedly since its 2002 founding but whose creative center has been Longstreth (composer, guitarist, vocalist). Based in Brooklyn from roughly 2005 onward (Williamsburg / Bushwick / Greenpoint at various points), they became the technical-virtuosity flagship of the late-2000s Brooklyn indie wave, particularly after *Bitte Orca* (2009) made the band's polyrhythmic guitar-and-vocal-harmony template a press fixation.

## Origins and Brooklyn geography

Longstreth founded Dirty Projectors in 2002 while he was an undergraduate at Yale, where he was studying composition and recording the band's earliest releases as essentially solo home-recordings. After graduating in 2005 he relocated to Brooklyn — Williamsburg first, later Bushwick, with periods of recording in upstate New York and elsewhere — and rebuilt the band as a Brooklyn-based ensemble. Singers Amber Coffman, Angel Deradoorian, and Haley Dekle (who joined in 2007) became central to the late-2000s lineup; the four-part harmonies they developed with Longstreth defined the band's sound through 2010.

The Brooklyn identification ran less neighborhood-specific than [TV on the Radio](./tv-on-the-radio.md)'s or [Grizzly Bear](./grizzly-bear.md)'s; Longstreth and the band rotated through enough lofts and apartments across Williamsburg, Bushwick, and Greenpoint that the band's geographic footprint reads as Brooklyn-broadly rather than block-specific.

## Sound and signature work

The band's defining characteristics are Longstreth's compositional ambition (intricate polyrhythms, unusual time signatures, melodies that move in non-pop ways), his vocal idiosyncrasy (a thin, high tenor with significant pitch-bend), the late-2000s lineup's three-part female vocal harmonies, and an overall sound that pushes guitar-band conventions toward art-music vocabulary while remaining recognizably indie rock.

### *Rise Above* (Dead Oceans, September 2007)

The album that drew sustained press attention. *Rise Above* is a track-by-track reimagining of Black Flag's 1981 hardcore-punk album *Damaged*, written from memory by Longstreth without revisiting the original. The conceptual gambit — and the unusual, polyrhythmic, harmony-stacked execution — made the band's compositional voice publicly legible for the first time.

### *Bitte Orca* (Domino, June 2009)

The breakthrough. The singles "Stillness Is the Move" and "Two Doves" got significant Pitchfork-and-NPR play; *Bitte Orca* was the band's commercial peak and the album that pulled them into the same conversation as [TV on the Radio](./tv-on-the-radio.md) and [Grizzly Bear](./grizzly-bear.md) as the defining Brooklyn indie acts of the year. The album was Pitchfork's #6 album of 2009 and made widely-distributed best-of-decade lists in subsequent years.

### Subsequent work

*Mount Wittenberg Orca* (2010, with Björk; based on a Housing Works concert from 2009), *Swing Lo Magellan* (2012, the band's last album with the late-2000s harmony lineup), and the self-titled *Dirty Projectors* (2017, written and recorded by Longstreth alone after the lineup dissolved) trace the band's evolution from ensemble to project. *Lamp Lit Prose* (2018) and the 5EPs / *Wing Dings* and *Windows Open* projects of the early 2020s extend the catalog with rotating collaborators.

## NYC venue history

The band's Brooklyn venue footprint included [Music Hall of Williamsburg](../../../neighborhoods/williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md), Brooklyn Masonic Temple, Bowery Presents loft programming, and a long list of [Bushwick](../../../neighborhoods/bushwick/overview.md) DIY spaces. On the Manhattan side, Mercury Lounge / Bowery Ballroom / Webster Hall ran the standard ladder; the band has played multiple Lincoln Center programs, including with the New York Philharmonic.

## Legacy

Dirty Projectors's contribution to the Brooklyn indie wave was specifically its compositional ambition. Where [Grizzly Bear](./grizzly-bear.md)'s contribution was harmony-and-arrangement craft and [TV on the Radio](./tv-on-the-radio.md)'s was racial and sonic expansion of the indie-rock template, Dirty Projectors pushed the wave's intellectual seriousness — explicitly engaging hardcore (*Rise Above*), R&B (*Bitte Orca*), African guitar music, and contemporary classical traditions in ways that broadened the audience's expectation of what an indie band could be doing.
