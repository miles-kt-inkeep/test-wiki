# TV on the Radio

TV on the Radio is an indie-rock band formed in [Williamsburg](../../../neighborhoods/williamsburg/overview.md), Brooklyn in 2001 by Tunde Adebimpe and David Sitek. With drummer Jaleel Bunton, bassist Gerard Smith (1974–2011), and Kyp Malone (vocals/guitar), they were the first widely-noticed indie-rock band whose entire creative life — apartment, rehearsal space, recording studio, immediate audience — ran in Brooklyn rather than Manhattan. Their 2003–2008 run produced the canonical Williamsburg-loft sound of the [post-punk revival](../overview.md)'s second wave.

## Origins and Brooklyn geography

Adebimpe and Sitek met in 1999 in a Williamsburg apartment building on N. 6th Street; Sitek was painting and Adebimpe (an animator and former NYU film student) was rehearsing. Sitek's apartment-studio (with the eventual name Stay Gold Studio) became the band's recording space and the place where most of *Desperate Youth, Blood Thirsty Babes* (2003 EP / 2004 LP) and *Return to Cookie Mountain* (2006) were tracked.

The band's geographic specificity — they recorded in Williamsburg, lived in Williamsburg, and built an audience at Williamsburg lofts and the original Northsix (later [Music Hall of Williamsburg](../../../neighborhoods/williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md)) — is a critical part of why they were the band the music press attached the "Brooklyn indie" descriptor to. Earlier post-punk-revival bands ([the Strokes](./strokes.md), [Interpol](./interpol.md), [LCD Soundsystem](./lcd-soundsystem.md)) were Manhattan-headquartered or commute-from-Brooklyn-to-Manhattan-show in identification; TV on the Radio was the first whose work was unambiguously Brooklyn-located.

## Sound and signature work

The band's sound combined doo-wop and gospel-leaning vocal harmonies (Adebimpe and Malone trading lead), distorted-rock instrumentation, electronic textures (drum loops, processed guitars, drone-leaning synth pads), and an unusually dense Sitek-produced mix that often pushed three or four melodic layers simultaneously. The result sat at an angle to the mid-2000s indie norm — too rhythmic and too R&B-leaning to fit the strict Joy Division / Television template Strokes-and-Interpol had set.

### *Desperate Youth, Blood Thirsty Babes* (Touch and Go, March 2004)

The debut LP. Released after the *Young Liars* EP (2003) and the band's "Staring at the Sun" single had drawn critical attention. The album won the inaugural Shortlist Music Prize that fall — the first explicit institutional recognition that a Brooklyn-based indie band could compete on national-album-of-the-year terms.

### *Return to Cookie Mountain* (4AD / Interscope, July 2006)

The breakthrough. David Bowie sang backing vocals on "Province"; the singles "Wolf Like Me" and "Province" defined the band's mid-decade peak. The album's heavier, more electronic textures pushed the post-punk-revival sound away from its Television-Joy Division origins and toward the dance-rock-and-art-rock hybrid that would define the late-decade Brooklyn indie wave.

### *Dear Science* (Interscope, September 2008)

The commercial peak. *Dear Science* was Pitchfork's album of the year for 2008 (a designation that, in 2008, was a meaningful career-shaping signal); the singles "Golden Age," "Dancing Choose," and "Halfway Home" got significant alternative-radio play. Production was lighter, brighter, and more pop-leaning than *Cookie Mountain*; Sitek had increasingly been working with other artists (Yeah Yeah Yeahs, Foals, Scarlett Johansson) and his external production credits were beginning to overlap with the band's own.

### Later

*Nine Types of Light* (2011) and *Seeds* (2014) extended the catalog. Bassist Gerard Smith died of lung cancer in April 2011, days after *Nine Types of Light*'s release. The band has remained active intermittently since.

## NYC venue history

TV on the Radio's pre-fame Brooklyn venue footprint included Northsix, Pete's Candy Store, the Knitting Factory's Brooklyn space, and various Williamsburg loft parties. Their Manhattan stage history runs through the standard Bowery Presents ladder — early Mercury Lounge sets, breakthrough Bowery Ballroom shows in 2004–2005, the larger Webster Hall and Terminal 5 by 2008. They have been frequent post-2007 [Music Hall of Williamsburg](../../../neighborhoods/williamsburg/venues/music-hall-of-williamsburg.md) bookings.

## Legacy

TV on the Radio's primary historical importance to New York music is twofold: as the band that opened the door for Brooklyn-bred indie rock in the post-punk-revival era (the bands that followed — [Grizzly Bear](./grizzly-bear.md), [Dirty Projectors](./dirty-projectors.md), Yeasayer, Animal Collective in their Brooklyn period — all built on the institutional space TV on the Radio created), and as the first significant Brooklyn-located rock band whose racial and sonic mix departed from the all-white guitar template of the Manhattan-side revival scene.
