# The Rapture

The Rapture was a four-piece dance-punk band that relocated to New York in 2001 and became, alongside [LCD Soundsystem](lcd-soundsystem.md), the flagship act of James Murphy and Tim Goldsworthy's DFA Records. Their 2002 DFA 12-inch "House of Jealous Lovers" — produced and remixed by Murphy and Goldsworthy in DFA's [Greenwich Village](../../../neighborhoods/greenwich-village/overview.md) studio — is the canonical dance-punk record: a seven-minute cowbell-driven hybrid of post-punk guitar, disco bass, and Luke Jenner's strangled falsetto that connected the early-2000s downtown revival back to the [Paradise Garage](../../../neighborhoods/soho/venues/paradise-garage.md) tradition the [electronic dance overview](../../electronic-dance/overview.md) describes.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Luke Jenner (vocals/guitar) and Vito Roccoforte (drums) formed the band in San Francisco in the late 1990s before moving east via Seattle. By 2001 they had landed in New York, recruited bassist Matt Safer, and connected with Murphy and Goldsworthy just as DFA was getting started. The label's first run of releases — the Rapture, [LCD Soundsystem](lcd-soundsystem.md), Black Dice, the Juan MacLean — established a house production aesthetic (live drums plus drum machine, heavy cowbell, ESG-meets-Liquid-Liquid grooves) that became identifiably "the DFA sound."

## Sound and signature work

"House of Jealous Lovers" (DFA, 2002) is the canonical text. The B-side "Sister Saviour" and the follow-up "Olio" cemented the formula. *Echoes* (DFA / Universal, 2003) was the major-label debut — co-produced by DFA — and remains the band's most-cited album, with "House of Jealous Lovers," "Olio," and the title track running the dance-punk template to its conclusion. *Pieces of the People We Love* (Vertigo, 2006) and *In the Grace of Your Love* (DFA, 2011) followed; the latter was a return to the DFA fold after a contentious major-label stretch and arrived just months after Matt Safer's departure. The band went on hiatus in 2014 and has reunited intermittently since.

## Manhattan venue history

- DFA Records, West 13th Street — production base for the early singles and *Echoes*.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — early-2000s breakout New York headlining shows.
- [Mercury Lounge](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) — pre-*Echoes* downtown sets.
- Webster Hall and Terminal 5 — peak-era touring stops.

## Legacy

The Rapture are the band the post-punk revival used to prove it could dance. Their DFA singles pulled the [Strokes](strokes.md)-[Interpol](interpol.md) leather-jacket consensus toward the [electronic dance](../../electronic-dance/overview.md) side of the room and gave Murphy and Goldsworthy their first production hit — without which DFA's larger 2000s output is hard to imagine. The dance-punk hybrid they perfected resurfaces in [LCD Soundsystem](lcd-soundsystem.md)'s catalog, in [Hercules & Love Affair](../../electronic-dance/artists/hercules-and-love-affair.md)'s disco revival, and in the [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](yeah-yeah-yeahs.md)' "Heads Will Roll." Jenner has continued solo work; Roccoforte and Safer have produced and performed elsewhere.
