# LCD Soundsystem

LCD Soundsystem is the project of producer, songwriter, and singer James Murphy, formed in New York in 2002 and operated as the flagship act of his and Tim Goldsworthy's DFA Records label. Across three studio albums in the original 2002–2011 run — *LCD Soundsystem* (2005), *Sound of Silver* (2007), and *This Is Happening* (2010) — Murphy distilled a specifically New York anxiety about scenes, aging, and authenticity into [electronic dance](../../electronic-dance/overview.md)-rock anthems. Although DFA was based in the West Village and Murphy lived between Manhattan and Brooklyn, the band's emotional geography is unmistakably Manhattan.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Murphy was a New York scene veteran by 2002 — a producer for Six Finger Satellite, a former wannabe sitcom writer, and a fixture at downtown bars and clubs. He and Tim Goldsworthy founded DFA Records in 2001, headquartered on West 13th Street in [Greenwich Village](../../../neighborhoods/greenwich-village/overview.md), and the label became the engine of New York's "dance-punk" wave alongside the Rapture and !!!.

The breakout single "Losing My Edge" (2002) was a long, deadpan monologue cataloguing record-collector anxiety — and a love letter / hate letter to the same downtown scene that had birthed [Sonic Youth](../../no-wave/artists/sonic-youth.md) and the [Beastie Boys](../../hip-hop/artists/beastie-boys.md). LCD Soundsystem became the live-band incarnation of Murphy's DFA studio work, with rotating members including Pat Mahoney, Nancy Whang, and Tyler Pope.

## Sound and signature work

*LCD Soundsystem* (2005) was the introduction — "Daft Punk Is Playing at My House," plus the singles compilation disc. *Sound of Silver* (2007) is the masterpiece: "All My Friends," "Someone Great," and "New York, I Love You but You're Bringing Me Down" weaponize Talking Heads-style rhythm machines for middle-aged downtown sentiment. *This Is Happening* (2010) followed with "Dance Yrself Clean" and "I Can Change."

The band staged a four-hour farewell at Madison Square Garden on April 2, 2011 — documented in the film *Shut Up and Play the Hits* — and disbanded. Five years later they reunited; *American Dream* (2017) topped the Billboard 200. Subsequent EPs and singles have continued sporadically.

## Manhattan venue history

- DFA Records HQ, West 13th Street — recording / nerve center for the band.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — early-career New York headlining.
- [Mercury Lounge](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) — multiple early shows.
- Webster Hall — [East Village](../../../neighborhoods/east-village/overview.md) tour stops in the 2000s and 2010s.
- Terminal 5 — multi-night residencies.
- Madison Square Garden — the famed April 2, 2011 farewell concert.

## Legacy

LCD Soundsystem reframed New York indie rock as something that could be self-aware about scenes, aging, and the city's mythology while still hitting like a great dance band. Murphy's debt to [Talking Heads](../../punk-rock/artists/talking-heads.md) and the DFA crew's debt to [no wave](../../no-wave/overview.md) and post-punk are both fully avowed. The band's New York-affirming, New York-mourning songbook is the soundtrack of mid-2000s Manhattan creative-class life — a direct ancestor to acts like [Vampire Weekend](vampire-weekend.md) and [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) who shared the same downtown air.

Murphy has also become a force in New York food and drink (the Williamsburg wine bar The Four Horsemen) and remains active as a producer.
