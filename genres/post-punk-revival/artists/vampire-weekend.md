# Vampire Weekend

Vampire Weekend is an American rock band formed at Columbia University in 2006 by singer-guitarist Ezra Koenig, multi-instrumentalist and producer Rostam Batmanglij, bassist Chris Baio, and drummer Chris Tomson. Their 2008 self-titled debut combined Paul Simon-style Afro-pop guitar, Western-classical strings, and lyrics about Cape Cod, Oxford commas, and Columbia campus life — a sound and persona that immediately divided critics. Five albums in, they are one of the most accomplished American indie-rock bands of the post-2000 era, and the most explicitly Manhattan-uptown act on this wiki.

## Formation and Manhattan context

Koenig, Batmanglij, Baio, and Tomson met as Columbia students between 2004 and 2006. Their first shows were on campus — Lerner Hall, Wien Lounge, and Columbia parties — making them one of the few notable bands of their era whose Manhattan geography centers on Morningside Heights rather than the [Lower East Side](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/overview.md). They initially gave their album away as a free Internet download in 2007, parlayed that into a deal with XL Recordings, and reissued it through XL in January 2008.

The band's worldview — half-ironic engagement with Ivy League WASP signifiers, sincere love of Africa-via-Paul-Simon Afro-pop, and Batmanglij's classical training — is rooted in that Columbia milieu. Once they left campus they relocated downtown, joining the larger [post-punk revival](../overview.md) ecosystem of [LCD Soundsystem](lcd-soundsystem.md) and [Yeah Yeah Yeahs](yeah-yeah-yeahs.md) without ever quite belonging to it sonically.

## Sound and signature work

*Vampire Weekend* (2008) — "A-Punk," "Oxford Comma," "Cape Cod Kwassa Kwassa" — was an instant and divisive sensation. *Contra* (2010) debuted at No. 1, propelled by "Cousins" and "Giving Up the Gun." *Modern Vampires of the City* (2013), the closing piece of an unofficial trilogy, won the Grammy for Best Alternative Music Album and is widely held to be their masterpiece — songs like "Step," "Hannah Hunt," and "Ya Hey" trade Afro-pop sparkle for darker, denser production.

Rostam Batmanglij left as a full member in 2016 (continuing as collaborator). *Father of the Bride* (2019) was a sprawling double album with a Haim-featuring lead single, "Harmony Hall." *Only God Was Above Us* (2024) returned them to a leaner sound and was widely praised as their best album in over a decade.

## Manhattan venue history

- Columbia University (Lerner Hall, Wien Lounge) — debut shows in 2006–2007.
- [Bowery Ballroom](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/bowery-ballroom.md) — first major NYC headlining gigs.
- [Mercury Lounge](../../../neighborhoods/lower-east-side/venues/mercury-lounge.md) — early breakout shows.
- Terminal 5 — multiple residencies during the *Contra* era.
- Madison Square Garden — sold-out shows from *Modern Vampires* onward.
- [Electric Lady Studios](../../../neighborhoods/greenwich-village/venues/electric-lady-studios.md) — recorded *Modern Vampires of the City* (2012–2013).

## Legacy

Vampire Weekend remains active and is one of the most critically respected American rock bands of the past two decades. Their willingness to import non-Western musical languages and to write openly about education, class, and faith is a meaningful counterweight to the in-character downtown nihilism of bands like [The Strokes](strokes.md) or [Interpol](interpol.md). Rostam's parallel career — solo records and production work for Carly Rae Jepsen, Hamilton Leithauser, Frank Ocean, and others — has made him one of the most influential producers of his generation.

In the Manhattan story they represent a rare uptown success story, a reminder that the city's music history is not exclusively a Bowery story.
